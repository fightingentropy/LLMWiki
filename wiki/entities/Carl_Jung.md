# Carl Jung

Swiss psychiatrist and analytical psychologist (1875–1961). Appears in [[Quotes]] for lines on addiction, the shadow, and why most people judge instead of think.

## Recurring lines (from [[Quotes]])

- **On addiction:** "All addictions are a low level search for God."
- **On repressed passions:** "A man who has not passed through the inferno of his passions has never overcome them. They then dwell in the house next door, and at any moment a flame may dart out and set fire to his own house. Whenever we give up, leave behind, and forget too much, there is always the danger that the things we have neglected will return with added force."
- **On thinking vs judging** (in Greek): "Είναι δύσκολο να σκέφτεσαι γιαυτό οι περισσότεροι άνθρωποι κρίνουν." ("It is difficult to think, which is why most people judge.")

## Why he's load-bearing in the wiki

- **Addiction framing** — the "low-level search for God" line sits alongside the [[Quotes]] observation that "addiction is a progressive narrowing of the things that bring you pleasure. A good life is a progressive expansion of the things that bring you pleasure." See [[Mental_Health]].
- **Shadow work / passing through the inferno** — matches [[Self_Improvement]]'s shame-as-fuel framing and the "only way out is through" bookmark in [[Youtube]] / [[Philosophy_and_Meaning]].
- **Thinking vs judging** — direct fit for the [[Self_Improvement]] thread on the enthusiasm gap and overthinking-as-judgment-substitute.

# GN3 Guidance Note 3

The primary exam and reference resource for inspection and testing of electrical installations. Published by the IET, GN3 provides guidance on how to perform inspection and testing work in compliance with BS 7671.

## Overview

**Full title**: Guidance Note 3: Inspection and Testing

**Publisher**: IET (Institution of Engineering and Technology)

**Edition referenced**: Fifth edition (as of 2026)

**Status**: Non-statutory guidance (but industry standard and primary exam resource)

**Exam use**: Open-book exam resource for Level 2 and Level 3 inspection and testing exams

## Structure and Chapters

### Chapter 1: General Requirements, Safety, and Competence

**Content**: Foundation material covering:
- Health and Safety at Work Act
- Electricity at Work Regulations
- Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations
- PUWER (Provision and Use of Work Equipment Regulations)
- Competence requirements
- Skilled/competent person definitions
- Safe working practices

**Importance**: Relevant to ALL exams - do not skip even if studying specific topics

### Chapter 2: Initial Verification

**Content**: Inspection and testing of new installations, including:
- Pre-verification requirements (information needed)
- Inspection methodology
- Test procedures for new work
- Dead-test sequence
- Live-test sequence
- Electrical Installation Certificate (EIC) completion
- Requirements for certification

**Application**: New circuits, rewires, consumer unit replacement, new installations

### Chapter 3: Periodic Inspection and Testing

**Content**: Condition reporting on existing installations:
- Periodic inspection methodology
- Visual assessment
- Test procedures for in-service installations
- Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) completion
- Risk-based inspection intervals
- Defect recording and reporting

**Application**: Inspection of installations in regular use, condition assessment

### Chapter 4: Test Instruments

**Content**: Detailed coverage of test equipment:
- Instrument types and selection
- Accuracy and calibration requirements
- Safety standards (BS EN 61010, BS EN 61557)
- GS38 guidance on test leads
- Category ratings (CAT II, CAT III, CAT IV)
- Specific equipment for different tests

**Importance**: Often underused by learners but important for both exams and practical work

### Chapter 5: Model Forms

**Content**: Complete example forms and documentation:
- Electrical Installation Certificate (EIC)
- Minor Electrical Installation Works Certificate (MEIWC)
- Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR)
- Schedules and supplementary forms
- PAT testing forms (V1-V6)
- Labels and tags

**Exam tip**: Fastest route to find information about form completion and signatory requirements - go directly to the form itself rather than searching text

**Practical use**: Templates for real work documentation

## Key Appendices

### Appendix A: Maximum Permissible Earth Fault Loop Impedance Values

**Content**: Table of maximum measured earth fault loop impedance values

**Important point**: Listed values are maximum measured = 80% of tabulated (disconnection time) values

**Use**: Live testing (not relevant for initial verification before supply connected)

**Reference**: Page reference provided during instruction

### Appendix B: Resistance Calculations and Conductor Values

**Content**: Tables showing:
- Conductor resistance values (milliohms per meter)
- Different conductor types and sizes
- Combined pair values for twin cables
- Twin-and-earth (T&E) cable CPC sizes (marked with *)

**Critical use**: Continuity testing calculations
- Formula: R = (mΩ/m × length) / 1000
- Examples: 10 mm² CPC, 0.75 mm² flex, T&E sizes

**Common trap**: Misinterpreting "line" and "protective" columns - think of them as "conductor one" and "conductor two"

**Page references**: Pages 182-183 as referenced in training

### Appendix C: Older Systems and Diverted Neutrals

**Content**: Guidance on older installation types and issues:
- Pre-BS 7671 installations
- Diverted neutral problems
- Assessment of aging systems

**Relevance**: More relevant to periodic inspection/testing (condition reporting) than initial verification

### Appendix D: Further Information on Specific Systems

**Content**: Additional guidance on specific installation types and scenarios

**Relevance**: Supplementary material for specific applications

## Table 1.1: Category Ratings

Listed in Chapter 4:
- CAT II: Building installation circuits
- CAT III: Distribution circuits
- CAT IV: Supply side/service entrance

**Exam relevance**: May feature in test equipment selection questions

**Revision tip**: Useful for highlighting and memorizing

## How to Use GN3 Effectively

### Study Approach

1. **Chapter 1 first**: Start with general requirements and competence
2. **Don't skip chapters**: All chapters have relevance even if title doesn't match specific exam
3. **Use Chapter 5 early**: Reference model forms to understand documentation requirements
4. **Appendices matter**: Key data in tables A and B especially

### Exam Preparation

**Open-book exam advantage**:
- Can refer to GN3 during exam
- Speed and knowing where to find information matters
- Highlighting key pages helpful (where permitted)
- Post-it notes for quick reference (where permitted)

**Search strategy**:
- Know chapter structure
- Don't assume "the answer" is in the chapter matching the exam title
- Chapter 1 applies to all exams
- Use Table of Contents and Index

### Common Exam Pitfall

**Searching Chapter 2** for all EIC information:
- Faster to go to Chapter 5 model forms
- Forms show exactly what's required
- Forms show the three signatories
- Forms show structure and requirements clearly

### Practical Work Reference

**Use GN3 in practical work**:
- Reference for test procedures and sequence
- Check appendices for calculation values
- Forms chapter for documentation requirements
- Chapter 1 for competence and safety requirements

## Cross-Reference to Other Standards

GN3 references and relates to:

**BS 7671**: Wiring Regulations (the actual technical requirements)
- GN3 explains BS 7671
- Use BS 7671 for detailed technical information
- GN3 for inspection/testing focused guidance

**On-Site Guide**: Practical electrical guidance
- Companion to GN3
- More casual/accessible style
- Both useful for understanding

**Building Regulations Approved Document P**: Part P (electrical)
- GN3 covers compliance assessment
- Building Regs control notifiable work

**HSE guidance** (GS38, HSG85, HSR25): Safety and regulations
- GN3 incorporates this guidance
- Test lead safety (GS38) important for Chapter 4

## Exam Context

- **Format**: 60-minute open-book multiple-choice
- **Questions**: 30 questions
- **Resource**: GN3 only (open-book)
- **Coverage**: All chapters relevant

### Level 3 Exams

- **Initial Verification Exam**: Focused on Chapter 2 but uses all chapters
- **Periodic Inspection/Testing Exam**: Focused on Chapter 3 but uses all chapters
- **Open-book**: Can refer to GN3

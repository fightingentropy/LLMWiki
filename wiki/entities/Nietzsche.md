# Friedrich Nietzsche

German philosopher (1844–1900). The most-quoted author in the human's [[Quotes]] collection — shows up around the edges of nearly every theme: morality, habit, aesthetics, power, self-overcoming.

## Recurring lines (from [[Quotes]])

- **On melody and teleology:** "The end of a melody is not its goal."
- **On self-command:** "He who cannot obey himself will be commanded." (appears twice in source — the human underlined it)
- **On fatigue:** "When a man is tired, he's attacked by ideas he conquered long ago."
- **On habit:** "The history of every day. — What is the history of every day in your case? Look at your habits that constitute it: are they the product of innumerable little cowardices and lazinesses or of your courage and inventive reason?"
- **On interpretation:** "All things are subject to interpretation. Whichever interpretation prevails at a given time is a function of power and not truth."
- **On outsider perception:** "And those who were seen dancing were thought to be insane by those who could not hear the music."
- **On aesthetic morality:** "If you crush a cockroach, you're a hero. If you crush a beautiful butterfly, you're a villain. Morals have aesthetic criteria."
- **On herd morality** (*Beyond Good and Evil*): the long passage on the softening of society to the point of siding with those who harm it — "herd morality, the morality of timidity, draws its ultimate consequence."

## Why he's load-bearing in the wiki

Nietzsche is the philosophical backbone of several wiki themes:

- **Habit as character** — "History of every day" maps directly to [[Self_Improvement]]'s compounding-delta principle (micro-choices, not catastrophic blowups).
- **Self-command** — "cannot obey himself will be commanded" is the ground under the agency/discipline threads in [[Self_Improvement]] and [[Masculinity_and_Men]].
- **Option-value of potential** — the human's own synthesis in [[Quotes]] extends Nietzsche's "humans have no intrinsic value but *optionality* of becoming" into Taleb's antifragility. See [[Nassim_Taleb]].
- **Interpretation-as-power** — echoes [[Political_Commentary]] and [[Philosophy_and_Meaning]] (storytelling as compression algorithm).
- **Fatigue attacks resolved ideas** — filed in [[Mental_Health]] alongside self-talk patterns.

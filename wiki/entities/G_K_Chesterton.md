# G. K. Chesterton

English writer, philosopher, Catholic apologist (1874–1936). Author of *Orthodoxy* and the Father Brown stories. Appears in the wiki for several aphorisms on love, compounding good acts, fairy tales, and feminism.

## Referenced in

- [[Quotes]] — four lines preserved by the human

## Lines in the source

> "The way to love anything is to realise that it might be lost." (from [[Quotes]])

> "Good and evil both increase at compound interest. That is why the little decisions you and I make every day are of such infinite importance. The smallest good act today is the capture of a strategic point from which, a few months later, you may be able to go on to victories you never dreamed of." (from [[Quotes]])

The compound-interest line maps directly to the micro-choices principle threaded through [[Self_Improvement]].

> On fairy tales: "What fairy tales give the child is his first clear idea of the possible defeat of bogey. The baby has known the dragon intimately ever since he had an imagination. What the fairy tale provides for him is a St. George to kill the dragon." (from [[Quotes]])

> "Feminism is a muddled idea that women are free when they serve their employers but slaves when they help their husbands." (from [[Quotes]])

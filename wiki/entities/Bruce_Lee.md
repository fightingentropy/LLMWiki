# Bruce Lee

Martial artist, actor, philosopher (1940–1973). Appears in [[Quotes]] for two distinct registers: self-talk as spellcasting, and the "be like water" adaptation framework.

## Recurring lines (from [[Quotes]])

- **On self-talk / spelling:** "Don't speak negatively about yourself, even as a joke. Your body doesn't know the difference. Words are energy and cast spells, that's why it's called spelling. Change the way you speak about yourself and you can change your life. What you're not changing, you're also choosing."
- **On the mind (comment on "Be Like Water"):** "Your mind is like a water my friend when it is agitated it becomes difficult to see but if you allow it to settle the answer becomes clear."
- **Core "Be Like Water":** "no matter what form water takes, it remains true to itself. It remains water."

- **Agitated-mind metaphor** — aligns with [[Mental_Health]] on overthinking paralysis; settling the mind as prerequisite for clear action.

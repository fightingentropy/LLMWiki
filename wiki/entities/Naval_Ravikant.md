# Naval Ravikant

Entrepreneur and aphorist (AngelList co-founder, @naval on Twitter). Appears in [[Quotes]] four times — the human clearly curates his one-liners on agency, freedom, and intention.

## Recurring lines (from [[Quotes]])

- **On intelligence:** "The only real measure of intelligence is if you can get what you want from life."
- **On intent:** "The people you most want to impress can read your intentions."
- **On a taste of freedom:** "Once you've truly controlled your own fate, for better or for worse, you'll never let anyone else tell you what to do. A taste of freedom can make you unemployable."
- **On projection:** "Ultimately, you attract the complement of what you project."

## Why he's load-bearing in the wiki

- **Intelligence = outcome, not IQ** — anchors [[Self_Improvement]]'s high-agency framing; aligns with "Most successful people I've met are not that smart. They just move fast, take risks and work a lot" elsewhere in [[Quotes]].
- **Freedom-as-unemployability** — fits the [[Philosophy_and_Meaning]] freedom-and-choice-architecture thread ("I've never come back from holiday feeling relaxed") and the [[Trading_Psychology]] observation that trading attracts people with "disdain for authority."
- **Intent is readable** — sits against the [[Dating_and_Relationships]] / [[Masculinity_and_Men]] theme that internal state leaks out regardless of performance.
- **Projection → attraction** — the "complement of what you project" line maps onto self-labeling ([[Self_Improvement]]) and on the [[Dating_and_Relationships]] attractor-state observation.

# Gabriel Garcia Marquez

Colombian novelist (1927–2014). Nobel laureate, author of *One Hundred Years of Solitude* and *Love in the Time of Cholera*. Appears in the wiki for two self-excavating passages on disorder, love, and generational nostalgia.

## Lines in the source

> "I discovered that my obsession for having each thing in the right place, each subject at the right time, each word in the right style, was not the well-deserved reward of an ordered mind but just the opposite: a complete system of pretence invented by me to hide the disorder of my nature … love is not a condition of the spirit but a sign of the zodiac." (from [[Quotes]])

> "The adolescents of my generation, greedy for life, forgot in body and soul about their hopes for the future until reality taught them that tomorrow was not what they had dreamed, and they discovered nostalgia." (from [[Quotes]], appears twice in source)

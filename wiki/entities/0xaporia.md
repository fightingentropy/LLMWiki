## Overview
Twitter: @0xaporia. Crypto trader known for rigorous, systems-oriented writing on variance, strategy hopping, and poker-style discipline. Also quoted Satoshi's 2010 production-cost passage in context of price-discovery evolution.

## Recurring Themes (from [[CT]])

### Poker player's variance discipline
> "Poker players grasp that they're dealt a fixed hand … They accept variance as part of the game. That restraint — knowing you don't need to play every hand — is a massive advantage." (from [[CT]])

### Strategy hopping & losing streaks
Central thesis: traders flee grueling nonlinear phases by hopping strategies, misreading noise as signal. Hard math on streak probabilities at 50% and 40% win rates:
> "Suppose a strategy has a 40% success rate … Over 100 trades, the probability of experiencing a losing streak of 8 or more is approx. 49%." (from [[CT]])

Principle: **Decouple Process from Outcome.**

### Price discovery proxies refining

### On profit-taking as self-sabotage
> The adage "no one ever went broke taking profits" is a (potentially) dangerous fallacy because it centers the exit decision on your entry price. It treats profit-taking as a psychological reward rather than a strategic forecast. For any strategy that doesn't win the majority of the time, outsized winners are the essential engine that funds your inevitable losses. By "locking in" a gain simply because your P&L is green, you are systematically underfunding the very outliers required for your survival. ... The market has no memory of your cost basis, and anchoring your decisions to it is a form of self-sabotage. (from [[X-Bookmarks]] #391)

@Cheshire_Cap followed with concrete EV math: if you win 5x on 20% of trades and lose 50% on 80% (EV +40%), aggressive TP at 2x flips you to EV -20% (from [[X-Bookmarks]] #392).

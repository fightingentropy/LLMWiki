# Heartiste

Pseudonymous game/red-pill writer (also published as **Roissy**). The single most-quoted external voice in the [[Women]] source file — anchor of the file's attraction framework and author of the canonical **Sixteen Commandments**.

## Voice

- Terse, aphoristic, polemical.
- Pro-"game" as a learnable craft; anti-feminist framing as a blind spot.
- Writes as if describing an observed biological order rather than prescribing one.

## The Sixteen Commandments (Roissy)

Preserved verbatim from [[Women]]:

1. **Never say "I love you" first** — don't rob her of the satisfaction of earning your love.
2. **Make her jealous** — jealousy excites; no girl wants a man no other woman wants.
3. **Your mission, not your woman, is your priority** — women want to subordinate themselves to a worthy purpose, not to be a man's everything.
4. **Don't play by her rules** — polarity is the core of a healthy relationship.
5. **Adhere to the golden ratio** — give her 2/3 of everything she gives you.
6. **Keep her guessing** — she doesn't want passion security; reward intermittently and unpredictably.
7. **Always keep two in the kitty** — options build confidence and boldness.
8. **Say you're sorry only when absolutely necessary** — apologies increase the demand for more. Two freebies for the life of the relationship.
9. **Connect with her emotions** — frolic in the surf of emotions rather than the arid desert of logic.
10. **Ignore her beauty** — train yourself out of awe; never compliment looks.
11. **Be irrationally self-confident** — irrational self-confidence out-pulls rational defeatism.
12. **Maximize your strengths, minimize your weaknesses** — there's a groupie for every male endeavor (except World of Warcraft).
13. **Err on the side of too much boldness** — inappropriate touch on date one beats no touch at all.
14. **Fuck her good** — own her body, own her gratitude, own her love.
15. **Maintain your state control** — "You are an oak tree. You will not be manipulated."
16. **Never be afraid to lose her** — fear is the love-killer.

See [[Dating_and_Relationships]] for how these interact with other frameworks in the wiki; see [[Gender_Dynamics]] for the background assumptions they encode.

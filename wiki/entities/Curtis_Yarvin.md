# Curtis Yarvin (Mencius Moldbug)

## Key Concept: Entropy vs. Extropy
Political philosophy reframed through thermodynamic metaphor (from February 2026 interview on Peter McCormack's podcast):

**Entropy (Left):** 
- Natural tendency toward disorder, decay, dissolution
- Deconstructs hierarchies, traditions, standards, borders, merit-based systems
- "Things can just decay"
- Historical example: "Cthulhu always swims left"

**Extropy (Right):**
- Active increase in order, complexity, intelligence, vitality, capability
- Life itself is extropic: builds organized structures (cells → organisms → societies)
- Strong hierarchies, sovereign decision-making (one person/small group who can actually act)
- Innovation creating new capability and complexity
- Patchwork-style governance or corporate-sovereign models that get things done

## Key Insight
Traditional "conservative vs. liberal" misses the point. Real right-wing energy isn't slow-motion leftism but **creative, order-building (extropic)**. Most "conservatives" are just slower versions of entropic trend.

The reframe: Politics = cosmic battle between decay and deliberate creation, not policy details.

## Reception
Described as "the best line that no one is mentioning." Sharp thermodyn metaphor that cuts through political noise.

Cuts through debate about specific policies to underlying worldview difference: Do you want to let systems rot, or actively build stronger structures?

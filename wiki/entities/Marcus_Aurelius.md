# Marcus Aurelius

Roman emperor (121–180 AD) and Stoic philosopher. Author of *Meditations*. Appears in [[Quotes]] primarily for lines on perception and mortality.

## Recurring lines (from [[Quotes]])

- **On perception:** "Everything we hear is an opinion, not a fact. Everything we see is a perspective, not the truth."
- **On being forgotten:** "Soon you will have forgotten all and all will have forgotten you." (echoed as "Someday you will have forgotten all and all will have forgotten you.")

## Why he's load-bearing in the wiki

- **Mortality as orienting device** — sits in the same cluster as [[Steve_Jobs]]'s "remembering that I'll be dead soon" and Sam Altman's subjective-time-compression argument (both in [[Quotes]]). The theme at the bottom of [[Quotes]]: "time as scarce resource — Altman, Jobs, Kornfield, Marcus Aurelius all converge."
- **Perception > correspondence truth** — slots next to the human's pragmatist thread in [[Philosophy_and_Meaning]] ("what's useful is more important than what is true") and Nietzsche's "all things are subject to interpretation."

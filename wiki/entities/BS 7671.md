# BS 7671

British Standard for electrical installation in buildings. The technical standard that defines requirements for electrical installations. Non-statutory guidance but industry standard that forms the basis for Building Regulations compliance.

## Overview

**Full title**: BS 7671 Requirements for electrical installations

**Standard body**: BSI (British Standards Institution)

**Status**: Non-statutory guidance (but references in Building Regulations, universal industry adoption)

**Relationship to GN3**: GN3 (Guidance Note 3) explains how to perform inspection and testing in compliance with BS 7671

**Relationship to Building Regulations**: Approved Document P references BS 7671 as the standard for electrical installations in buildings

## How BS 7671 is Used

### In Design

Electrical designers use BS 7671 to:
- Plan and design installations to comply
- Select appropriate protection systems
- Size cables and conductors properly
- Ensure safety features are integrated

### In Installation

Installers use BS 7671 to:
- Understand design intentions
- Install to required standards
- Know protection requirements
- Ensure connections and routing comply

### In Inspection and Testing

Inspectors use BS 7671 to:
- Understand what to check
- Know pass/fail criteria
- Assess compliance with installation requirements
- Verify protection systems function

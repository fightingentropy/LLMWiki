# Psychology & Behavior

## Reading as Emotional Systematization
"Reading is systematizing the way you feel." Converting emotional experience into language creates structure and understanding.

## Compounding Tiny Deltas
"Power of replacing tiny bad decisions (-1, -3) with tiny good ones (+1, +2). Life = accumulation of micro-choices."

Example: Basketball—miss layup, get frustrated, give up wide-open 3. Score only -3 but expected value is +2 → actual swing is -5. Repeat a few times and you're down -40 (Suns in Luka Game 7).

**No catastrophic blowup; slow erosion or accumulation through daily inflection points.**

## ADHD & Friendship
"One thing I love about ADHD people: no friendship degradation mechanics. Can text after months/years radio silence and pick right back up."

## Cognitive Dissonance & Affect
"A brain properly maintaining homeostasis is incapable of hyper-productivity" (Asimov 500 books, Edison 1093 patents, Van Gogh 2100 artworks, Mozart 600 compositions).

Such output requires **cognitive dissonance**—discomfort maintained as fuel.

## Overthinking vs. Acting
"All miserable people: they overthink and underact."

System to escape cold, dark prison of overthinking: **just act**. Imperfect action > perfect planning.

## Proximity & Attraction
"Your coworker isn't hot, they're just within 10ft of you 40hrs/week" = [[Proximity_Infatuation]]. Context creates perceived attraction.

## Psychedelics & False Insight
"Psychedelics make you feel like you're learning something new/deep, but your consciousness is extremely fallible. It's like déjà vu—brain's familiarity circuit misfiring in unfamiliar context."

**Mechanism behind 'false insight' is same as therapeutic potential.** If aimed at genuine psychological blocker (trauma, forgiveness), the "false insight" sticks emotionally. Like LLM hallucinations—whether insight is "false" depends on whether it attaches to ground truth.

## Fear of Physical Defense
"People who can't defend themselves physically (women, low-T men) parse information through consensus filter as safety mechanism. Don't ask 'is this true?'—ask 'will others be OK with me thinking this?'"

**Only high-T alpha males and neurodivergent people (autists) are actually free to parse information objectively.** Republic of free thinkers vs. democracy of consensus prisoners.

## Relationships & Validation
"You fall in love with people who make you love the person you are when you're around them."

"My therapist once said: 'People don't abandon people they love, they abandon people they're using.' That was all closure I needed."

## Gender Observations
Women trained to equate existence with perception. Hence obsessive documentation (selfies, food photos, stories)—not male gaze, but **female narcissism and pleasure in watching selves look hot.**

Men watch porn, women watch mirrors.

## Words as Reality Anchors
"Words are like spells. Be careful labeling yourself 'traumatized' or 'depressed'—they start becoming real."

Language rewires neurology. Self-labeling creates pathways.

## Depression Origins
"Chemical imbalance theory conclusively disproven. What causes depression? Learned helplessness. Not leaving house, no socializing, high dopamine baseline (social media), lack sunlight, poor diet/sleep."

## Trading Psychology
Why people trade:
- Greatest game with millions of variables, no single optimal way
- Fever dream of always-just-out-of-reach puzzle
- Arrogance + authority issues (no boss) + autonomy + asymmetric upside
- Line of traders (family destiny)
- Performative, endless, introspective, disciplinary, humbling, asymmetrically rewarding

## High-Agency People
"Single most important trait in friends, coworkers, partners, kids: high agency."

## Genius & Madness
Intuitive decision-makers thrive plugged in and on top of game, making preternaturally good calls. As they age and lose info flow, confident inferences go awry. Why genius and madness ride closely; why Nobel laureates, Hughes, Elon go insane over time. Measured thinkers stay safer.

## Hope vs. Hopelessness
Famous Richter rat study (1957): Domesticated rats swim 40-60 hours; wild rats die in 15 minutes in same conditions. But if wild rats exposed to water multiple times and removed, they last as long as domesticated rats.

**Not physiological—psychological. Hopelessness kills; hope sustains.** If escape seems possible, endurance multiplies.

## Authenticity vs. Radical Honesty
"Radical honesty is lazy. Ability to discern between necessary disclosure and tactful omission = important life skill." Context matters.

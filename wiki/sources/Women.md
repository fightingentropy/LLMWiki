## Core Theses (the human's recurring framings)

### 1. Male vs Female Cognitive Asymmetry
Opens the file with a long @minordissent passage:

> To men, especially autistic men, actions are disposable and inconsequential… To women, EVERY action matters, EVERY action has a deeper story, a deeper moral weight… "Only someone who believes X could possibly do Y."

Men track first-order effects and apply second-order thinking to systems (politics, power, business); women apply second-order thinking to people directly (and are "generally unable to apply it effectively to systems"). Men "hallucinate" in the same shape women do — just pointed at macro systems ("blame it on the jews") rather than at the boyfriend who cried during a movie.

Corollary: men's political diversity isn't intellectual superiority — it's that biology doesn't hand men a single vocation, whereas for women biology dictates mothering. "Women are born valuable. Men must become valuable." Both double-edged.

### 2. Motherhood as All-Consuming Trajectory Change
Extended essay justifying modern fertility decline. Becoming a mother is "an all-consuming and irreversible trajectory change" — physically handicapped for ~2 years, utterly dependent on husband/provider, decade-long earnings gap, etc. Even with 90% chance of 20x return, few would take the bet; with realistic divorce-rate numbers, effectively no one. Women's politics revolving around abortion rights is framed as rational given this calculus — the same way men's politics revolves around conscription, family courts, taxation, replacement.

### 3. Women as Memoryless / Present-Tense / Shape-Shifting
- "Women are basically memoryless creatures who run entirely on how you're making them feel in the moment."
- "You can't build equity with women. Every day is like a brand new day with them. There's no past, nor future. Only now."
- "Women are shape shifters, they will literally change their whole identity and persona based off the guy they're into or dating." Suddenly vegetarian, suddenly loves football, suddenly prefers country music.
- "Chicks are intuitive like dogs."
- "Women…are better grounded in the present" — flipped as the one thing women do better than men (vs. ambitious dudes living in a "nebulous future").

Contradiction flag: elsewhere "A woman's love lasts until she finds a better suitor" and "women spend half the relationship getting over it before they leave" imply forward-looking emotional planning, not pure presentism.

# Business & Finance

## Consumer Power & Gender
Women control or influence **85% of consumer spending** in the U.S. and ~$31.8 trillion globally. They make majority of household purchasing decisions (groceries, homes, cars). Economy propped up by female consumption; billionaires watch their dollars closer than average 20yo in big city.

## Market Psychology
"When news impacts price, market participants fixate on whether it's true or not. **More often, actual veracity is immaterial.** It's how the market reacts to news and for how long that's informative."

**Markets are 6-8 months ahead of the news.** Price moves first, "why" follows. Stocks peak when best has been discounted; fundamentals deteriorate later.

## Capital Timing
"You act too quickly when trying to grow capital, and you act too slowly when trying to protect capital." [[Capital_Allocation_Timing]]

## Reflexive Pumps & Supply Shocks
"Reflexive pumps don't begin with wave of buyers; they start with **no one left willing to sell**. Stubborn holder base creates synthetic supply shock. Shorts pile in. Disbelief rally erupts from sideline capital. FOMO chases into thin air. Parabola and attention games begin."

## The Catfish Effect
Chinese business strategy: introduce managed competition (not to destroy, but to activate innovation). Tesla in China = government-curated disruption. Unlike Western natural market disruption, this is **managed agitation—stress on purpose because stagnation is bigger risk**. [[Catfish_Effect]]

## Trading Wisdom
- **Cash = option** → Option value maximized by volatility → Inflation is option premium
- Crypto/Bitcoin volatility exposure creates capital allocators equipped for generations
- High-timeframe resistance matters; ATH corrections driven by short-term acute flows ($10B+) overwhelming passive flows ($500m+ ETF buying)

## Market Cycles & Seasonality
- **January onwards:** Seasonally strong trading activity
- **May-August:** "Sell in May and Go Away" period; low volume, especially week after July 4
- **Late August-December:** Most of the year's trading happens; low volume except for FOMC meetings (wild gyrations)
- **Post-Thanksgiving:** Lull before year-end
- **Late December:** Low activity until New Year
- **September:** Trading year really begins; FOMC meeting historically volatile

## Equity Valuation Breakdown
- **Book Value** - What's real
- **Discounted Earnings (3yr)** - What's likely
- **Everything else** - Hopes & Dreams

"Way to go before Hopes & Dreams turn into Despair & Nightmares."

## Bear Market Phases (Robert Rhea, 1935)
1. **Abandonment of hopes** - Bull market's basis crumbles
2. **Reduced earning power** - Dividends fall
3. **Distress liquidation** - Must-sell securities for living expenses

Each phase divided by secondary reaction (bear market rally), often mistaken for bull return.

## Perpetual Futures
Best product-market fit in crypto. Robert Shiller (1992) proposed perpetual futures for illiquid assets (homes, labor costs, CPI). Creates derivatives markets for hard-to-price assets.

## Currency & Pricing
"You can make things cheap and it will fix lots of problems. But weird things happen at $0.00."

Singapore's healthcare strategy: when free, demand booms and destroys system (NHS waiting lists = 9mo-1yr, 7M people waiting). San Francisco made community college free → scammers filled slots. **Marginal cost near zero breaks incentive structures.**

## Distinctiveness & Competition
From Bezos' final shareholder letter: "The world wants you typical. Democracies aren't normal—tyranny is historical norm. It takes continuous work to maintain distinctiveness. The world pulls at you in 1000 ways. Don't let it happen."

All companies face pressure to merge into equilibrium with environment. Amazon's challenge: stay distinctive despite entropy pull.

## Tokenization & Asset Management
- Public/private securities currently held in segregated databases managed by banks/brokers
- Tokenization enables: 24/7/365 global trading, seamless collateralization, non-custodial lending, broader investor access
- Most benefit those without robust existing market structure
- Larry Fink (BlackRock) very interested in tokenization thesis

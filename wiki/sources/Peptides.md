# Peptides

Source file: `raw/misc/Peptides.md`. A practical, how-to **Retatrutide Reconstitution Guide** — taking lyophilized (freeze-dried) peptide powder to a measured subcutaneous injection. The entire document is about a single compound, [[Retatrutide]] (LY3437943), but most of the procedural knowledge (reconstitution, dilution math, storage, injection technique) generalizes to other injectable research peptides — captured in [[Peptide Reconstitution]].

> ⚠️ The source opens with an explicit disclaimer: retatrutide is an **investigational compound** in Phase 3 trials, **not FDA-approved**, and the guide is "for educational and research reference purposes only." Preserved here as a personal reference note, not medical advice.

## Key Takeaways

- **Reconstitution material matters.** Use [[Bacteriostatic Water]] (BAC water, 0.9% benzyl alcohol preservative), **not** sterile water, saline, or tap water. BAC water allows a reconstituted vial to be stored and drawn from for up to **28 days**; sterile water (no preservative) forces use within 24 hours.
- **The "most popular setup": 10 mg vial + 2 mL BAC water = 5 mg/mL.** At that concentration, dose-to-units mapping is clean: 1 mg = 20 units, 2 mg = 40 units, 4 mg = 80 units on a U-100 insulin syringe (100 units = 1 mL).
- **Dilution math.** Concentration (mcg/mL) = peptide amount (mcg) ÷ water added (mL). Units to inject = desired dose (mcg) ÷ concentration (mcg/mL) × 100. Example: 10,000 mcg vial + 2 mL = 5,000 mcg/mL; a 4 mg dose = 4,000 ÷ 5,000 × 100 = **80 units**.
- **Handle gently — the failure modes are mechanical.** Never shake (foaming destroys peptide bonds, –30–50% potency); aim water at the glass wall, not the powder; mix by slow rolling/swirling; solution should go clear and colorless in 1–3 min. Cloudy or particulate → discard.
- **Storage.** Lyophilized powder is stable for months (room temp) to years (fridge/freezer). **Reconstituted** solution: refrigerate 2–8 °C, use within **28 days**; **never freeze** reconstituted peptide (ice crystals shear the bonds); never leave at room temp.
- **Injection.** Subcutaneous only, once weekly on the same day, rotating sites (abdomen / front thigh / back of upper arm). Let the cold vial reach room temp 15–30 min before drawing to reduce sting.
- **Titrate, don't jump.** Clinical schedule ramps 1 → 2 → 4 → 6 → 9 → 12 mg over ~21+ weeks. Starting high causes severe GI side effects; hold at a step an extra 2–4 weeks if side effects are significant.

## Common Mistakes (from the source)

- Shaking the vial → denatures peptide, –30–50% potency
- Spraying water directly onto powder → damages molecular structure
- Sterile water instead of BAC water → no preservative, 24-hour window
- Leaving reconstituted solution at room temp → ~10–20% potency loss per day
- Freezing reconstituted solution → ice crystals destroy bonds
- Reusing needles → contamination + dull needle
- Too little water → ultra-concentrated, can't measure small doses accurately

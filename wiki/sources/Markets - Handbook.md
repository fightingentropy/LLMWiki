## Commodities

### General Principles
Commodities are boom & bust cycles driven by supply & demand. Cyclical in nature.

### Oil Futures
**Key Factors**:
- OPEC decisions and production levels
- Geopolitical events and supply disruptions
- GDP growth and economic activity
- Policy changes (renewable energy adoption, etc.)

### Natural Gas
**Key Factors**:
- Production from major gas fields
- Storage levels
- Decisions by major producers
- Weather (colder months = more demand)
- Liquefied natural gas exports
- Energy policies and renewable competition

### Gold
**Key Factors**:
- Investor sentiment and risk appetite
- Safe-haven demand in economic uncertainty
- Interest rates (higher rates = lower attractiveness)
- DXY (US Dollar Index) - low rates or weak dollar = higher gold attractiveness
- Central bank buying
- Jewelry demand
- Mining supply

### Silver
**Key Factors**:
- Industrial demand (electronics, solar panels)
- Investor/ETF flows (safe-haven and speculation)
- Interest rates and real yields
- DXY correlation
- Gold-Silver ratio (relative value)
- Mining and recycling supply (often by-product of base metals mining)
- Futures positioning and volatility

### Orange Juice (OJ) Futures
**Key Factors**:
- Weather conditions and forecasts
- Crop diseases and supply shocks
- Supply and demand shifts
- Trade policies and tariffs

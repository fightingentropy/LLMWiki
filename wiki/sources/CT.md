## Price Discovery & Market Mechanics

### Satoshi on production cost → price (2010)
(from [[Satoshi_Nakamoto]], quoted by [[0xaporia]])
> "The price of any commodity tends to gravitate toward the production cost. If the price is below cost, then production slows down. If the price is above cost, profit can be made by generating and selling more. At the same time, the increased production would increase the difficulty, pushing the cost of generating towards the price."

### Proxies refining into precise expressions
[[thiccythot]]:
> "its interesting how price discovery moves from blunt proxies to more precise expressions with each wave. first wave → buy any oil → wti outperforms brent bc its the default proxy. second wave → better read on mechanics of the supply shock → brent cleaner expression than wti. you see it with crypto too with each cycle becoming more selective with asset quality … it's so cool to witness adaptiveness of the system, feels like modern art"

### Reflexivity

### Cascades as secondary effect
[[fewseethis]]:
> "the simple truth is that cascades occur as a RESULT of prices going down within a levered environment. in other words a cascade is a secondary effect. then — outside of a mechanical bounce — prices can resume wherever they were going in the first place: up or down."

### HYPE-margined collateral risk
> "The risk is in possible over collateralization of positions using HYPE. USDC has a 1:1 value and can be infinitely redeemed in a liquidation for its full value. On the other hand, a HYPE margined position to maintain a precise notional value is more complicated. When liquidated, HYPE is a coin with an FDV that is not backed 1:1 by USDC, and that position may need to be sold into USDC. You create a risk of collateralizing too many positions in HYPE and a cascade could send HYPE low enough where slippage and spread leave positions UNDER collateralized and the protocol insolvent."

## Trading Psychology & Bag-Holding

### Awkward bag-holding test
[[CryptoCred]]:
> "The price action of the last week leads to awkward bag-holding. You don't want to sell because it's already down a lot and/or you're tempted to wait for a bounce to reassess. Simple test: 'If I didn't have a position, would I want the exposure I have now?' Yes → Cool, you're fine. No → Do something about it. Active positions cost mental capital, which is a scarce resource."

### Poker analogue
[[0xaporia]]:
> "Poker players grasp that they're dealt a fixed hand and operate in a controlled environment, so they accept variance as part of the game. It allows them to fold strategically and avoid the trap of forcing action when odds or context are unfavorable. That restraint — knowing you don't need to play every hand — is a massive advantage over people who start out with trading. Novice traders often conflate constant activity with progress. Poker players know this not to be true."

### Strategy hopping + losing streaks
[[0xaporia]]:
> "Strategy hopping is one of the most under discussed issues on CT imo. It's mostly a flight from the grueling, nonlinear phase where most progress happens … Suppose a strategy has a 50% success rate — a coin flip. Over 100 trades, the probability of experiencing a losing streak of 5 or more is approx. 80% … of 7 or more is approx. 32% … Now suppose your strategy has a 40% success rate … of 5 or more is approx. 98% … of 8 or more is approx. 49% … Losing streaks are not rare events. Yet, humans are terrible at internalizing this. **Decouple Process from Outcome.**"

## Contradictions (flagged, not resolved)

- **Crypto ingenuity vs degradation** — [[DegenSpartan]] explicitly contrasts the "ingenuity/self-sovereignty" framing against his own "degradation/greed" framing. Same asset class, opposite theses.
- **Narrative reading** — [[CL207]] says narratives are hallucinated *ex post* from price; @lightcrypto says narratives are needed as schelling points *ex ante*. Both partially true.
- **Tools vs instinct** — [[CryptoCred]]'s microstructure maximalism vs @MatriXBT's pure-instinct claim.
- **Short-term activity** — Lord Ashdrake / [[cobie]] on "sit on your ass" vs [[GiganticRebirth]]'s "sacrifice your weekends to harvest yield." Depends on life stage and regime.

# Market Cycles

Recurring boom-bust and seasonal patterns in markets — with a heavy crypto bent in this wiki. The frame: cycles are driven by dilution vs. inflow dynamics, participant composition (PvP vs. PvE), and psychological oscillation between idealism and cynicism.

## In this wiki

Referenced from [[Crypto Trading]] and threaded through the [[CT]] trader commentary. Not one neat model — the wiki holds several overlapping frames (Rhea's 1935 bear phases, cobie's supercycle, GCR's inverse supercycle) without resolving them.

## Key themes

- **Dilution kills bulls.** [[Pentosh1]]: "The end simply comes when the dilution exceeds the amount of capital inflows" (from [[CT]]).
- **Supercycle vs. inverse supercycle.** [[cobie]] sees crypto as a decade-long supercycle bubble; [[GCRClassic]] sees the inverse — crypto going to 0 as gen-z chases memes over growth stocks (both from [[CT]], [[Crypto Trading]]).
- **PvP vs. PvE regimes.** [[Rewkang]]: crypto cycles alternate between trader-dominant (PvP) and retail-paycheck-DCA (PvE) regimes. Mean-reversion heuristics that work in PvP break in PvE ([[CT]]).
- **First-cycle psychology.** [[CryptoCred]]: first-cycle participants make money via open-minded optimism, lose it for the same reason. Cycles reward alternation between childlike idealism and sagely cynicism.
- **Bear-market structure (Rhea, 1935).** Three phases: abandonment of hopes → reduced earning power → distress liquidation. Each separated by a secondary reaction often mistaken for a new bull ([[Bizz_and_Finance]], [[Markets]]).
- **Seasonality overlay.** January-October strong; May-August "sell in May"; week after July 4 is the annual nadir; September is when the year really begins; post-Thanksgiving lull ([[Markets]], [[Trading_Psychology]]).
- **Reflexive pumps.** Cycles end and begin with reflexivity — no one left to sell, shorts pile in, disbelief rally, FOMO parabola ([[Markets]]).

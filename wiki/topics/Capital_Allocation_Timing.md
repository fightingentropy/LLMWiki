# Capital Allocation Timing

The observation that most capital-allocation errors stem from a single asymmetry in speed: investors act too quickly when trying to grow capital and too slowly when trying to protect it.

## In this wiki

Introduced in [[Bizz_and_Finance]] as a one-line heuristic and echoed inside [[Trading_Psychology]]. The phrase frames panic selling and hesitant buying as two faces of the same timing error rather than separate failure modes.

## Key themes

- **The asymmetry.** "You act too quickly when trying to grow capital, and you act too slowly when trying to protect capital" (from [[Bizz_and_Finance]]). This single inversion explains most losses.
- **Growth phase vs. protection phase.** [[Business_Strategy]] reframes it as lifecycle: growth phase wants speed over precision, protection phase wants security over opportunity. Most entrepreneurs reverse these instinctively.
- **Cash as optionality.** Cash = optionality; option value maximized by volatility; inflation is the option premium ([[Trading_Psychology]], [[Markets]]). Holding cash through 40%+ alt wicks preserves the ability to deploy.
- **Seasonality overlay.** Capital-timing errors compound with market seasonality — low-volume summer periods and September FOMC gyrations punish ill-timed deployment ([[Trading_Psychology]]).
- **Opportunity-window quote.** "You need to take advantage of an opportunity of a lifetime in the lifetime of the opportunity" — Thomson (from [[Quotes]], via [[Trading_Psychology]]).

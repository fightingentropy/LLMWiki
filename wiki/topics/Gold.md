## Overview
Gold is a macro asset and safe-haven that acts as insurance in portfolios. Price driven by real rates, USD, and risk sentiment.

## Key Drivers

### Macro Factors
- **Real Yields**: Higher real rates = lower gold (opportunity cost of holding zero-yield asset)
- **USD Index**: Weak dollar = higher gold (cheaper in foreign currencies)
- **Risk Sentiment**: Increases in risk-off periods (safe-haven flow)
- **Economic Uncertainty**: Flight to quality and insurance value

### Fundamental Drivers
- **Central Bank Buying**: Official sector demand for reserves
- **Jewelry Demand**: Consumption and adornment
- **Mining Supply**: Primary gold production and recycling
- **Investment Flows**: ETF and fund positioning

### Technical Drivers
- **Positioning**: Commodity futures positioning can drive sharp moves
- **Technicals**: Support/resistance levels drive mechanical flows
- **Options**: Gamma exposure and vol spikes

## Trading Relationships
- **Inverse real rates**: Rising real rates (especially unexpected) = gold down
- **Inverse USD**: Strong dollar (especially risk-off dollar) = gold down
- **Positive correlation with risk-off**: When equities fall, gold typically rallies

## Scenarios
- **Recession**: Real rates fall, gold rallies (terminal rate lower)
- **Stagflation**: Real yields under pressure, gold rallies despite inflation
- **Deflation**: Real yields spike, gold vulnerable (but competing with nominal yields)
- **Growth acceleration**: Real rates rise, gold under pressure

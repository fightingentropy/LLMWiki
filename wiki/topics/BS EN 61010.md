# BS EN 61010

**BS EN 61010 — "Safety requirements for electrical equipment for measurement, control, and laboratory use"**. The core safety standard for the test instruments themselves — multimeters, multi-function testers, PAT testers, clamp meters, insulation testers. It is the IEC 61010 series adopted as a British / European standard.

Where [[GS38]] governs the leads and probes you attach, 61010 governs the instrument body, its internal construction, its user protection, and — critically — its **CAT rating**.

## What the Standard Covers

From the source notes: *"BS EN 61010 (IEC 61010) — Covers: electrical safety of test equipment, design and construction safety, protection against hazards, compliance with safety principles. Requirement: all test equipment should comply with this standard."*

Concretely:

- Protection against electric shock to the operator
- Protection against mechanical hazards, spread of fire, excessive temperature
- Insulation coordination — creepage and clearance distances inside the instrument
- Input protection — fusing, transient withstand, behaviour under fault
- Markings, symbols, and user documentation
- **Overvoltage categories (CAT ratings)** combined with voltage ratings

## CAT Ratings (Overvoltage / Measurement Categories)

The CAT rating tells you where on an installation the instrument is safe to connect. A CAT rating is always paired with a voltage (e.g. **CAT III 600 V**, **CAT IV 1000 V**). Higher category = greater expected transient energy, so stricter internal design.

- **CAT I** — protected electronic circuits isolated from the mains (e.g. signal-level electronics in a device). No direct mains connection. Rarely appropriate for an electrician's work.
- **CAT II** — single-phase receptacle-connected loads at more than about 10 m from a CAT III source, or about 20 m from a CAT IV source. Example: appliances, portable tools, loads plugged into a socket.
- **CAT III** — fixed installation circuits: distribution boards, fixed wiring, final circuits at the distribution board, motor control centres. This is the typical category for domestic/commercial distribution work.
- **CAT IV** — origin of the installation: meter tails, service entrance, supply side of the main isolator, incoming overhead/underground supply, metering. Highest transient stress.

The source notes summarise practical selection:

*"Using CAT II equipment on CAT IV circuit = potential hazard. Using CAT IV equipment on CAT II circuit = overkill but safe. Know what you're testing before selecting equipment."*

## Voltage Ratings

The voltage paired with the CAT (e.g. 600 V, 1000 V) is the maximum steady-state working voltage to earth. The standard then defines the transient overvoltage the instrument must withstand at that category.

Rule of thumb: always check **both** the category **and** the voltage. A "CAT III 300 V" meter is not safe for a 400 V three-phase distribution board even though the category is right.

## Instrument Markings

Compliant instruments carry:

- CE / UKCA mark
- The **CAT rating** and **voltage** (usually near each input terminal)
- Double-square "class II" symbol where applicable
- Reference to IEC/BS EN 61010 in the manual and on the label

## Relationship to Other Standards

- [[GS38]] — the HSE guidance for the **leads and probes** plugged into the 61010-compliant instrument.
- [[BS EN 61557]] — **performance / accuracy** of instruments used to verify installation parameters. A multifunction tester needs both: 61010 for safety, 61557 for accuracy.

Source note: *"Test equipment should meet standards covering safety and accuracy: 61010 (safety), 61557 (accuracy), GS38 (lead/probe condition)."*

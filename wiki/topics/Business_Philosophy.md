# Business Philosophy

Cross-cutting principles about how businesses create and preserve value — distinct from tactical [[Business_Strategy]]. Draws on Bezos, Chinese industrial policy, and the wiki's entropy/extropy political frame.

## In this wiki

Referenced from [[Culture]] as the home for the Catfish Effect framing. Sparse as a standalone topic — most business content lives in [[Business_Strategy]] and [[Markets]]. This page collects the philosophical overlays (distinctiveness, managed competition, stagnation-as-risk).

## Key themes

- **Managed competition, not disruption.** China's [[Catfish_Effect]]: introduce curated outside pressure (Tesla in China) to activate domestic complacency. Differs fundamentally from Western disruptive-innovation frameworks — not destruction, but deliberate agitation because stagnation is the bigger risk ([[Culture]], [[Bizz_and_Finance]]).
- **Distinctiveness as active work.** Bezos' final shareholder letter: "The world wants you typical. Democracies aren't normal — tyranny is historical norm. It takes continuous work to maintain distinctiveness. The world pulls at you in 1000 ways" ([[Bizz_and_Finance]], [[Business_Strategy]]).
- **Entropy pull on organizations.** All companies face pressure to merge into equilibrium with their environment. Amazon's challenge: stay distinct despite the pull.
- **Bussin company, sus price.** "Buy a bussin company at a sus price vs. a sus company at a bussin price" — fundamentals beat entry price, but entry price sets margin of safety ([[Bizz_and_Finance]]).
- **Growth phase vs. protection phase.** Act quickly to grow, slowly to protect — most reverse these ([[Capital_Allocation_Timing]]).
- **Parallels to extropy.** The distinctiveness-as-active-work frame mirrors [[Curtis_Yarvin]]'s extropy — organized complexity requires energy input or decays. See [[Entropy_vs_Extropy]].

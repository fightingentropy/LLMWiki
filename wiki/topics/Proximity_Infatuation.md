# Proximity Infatuation

The observation that sustained physical proximity manufactures perceived attraction independent of actual compatibility. Named explicitly in the raw sources as "proximity infatuation" — the coworker-crush effect generalized.

## In this wiki

Referenced inline in [[Psychology]] and echoed in [[Women]]. The meme form: "Your coworker isn't hot — they're just within 10ft of you 40hrs/week" (via [@Nia_mp4](https://x.com/Nia_mp4/status/1869907388557340785)).

## Key themes

- **Context creates perceived attraction.** Sustained proximity produces what feels like chemistry but is actually repeated exposure ([[Psychology]]).
- **Kryptonite for women specifically.** "You can have the most profoundly intricate connection for six months, disappear for two weeks, and she's already met a guy who's made her feel everything she felt with you in the span of a day. Women are very fickle, very in the moment. Proximity is their kryptonite" (from [[Women]]).
- **Implication for dating.** Being in the room matters more than being the right person. Geographic/venue choice is upstream of romantic outcomes.
- **Implication for self-knowledge.** Feelings attributed to a specific person may be feelings attributed to a specific schedule and space.
- **Connects to geographic determinism.** Same mechanism powers the [[Philosophy_and_Meaning]] claim that you're the average of the 5 places, 5 routines, and 5 inputs you're exposed to most — not the 5 people.

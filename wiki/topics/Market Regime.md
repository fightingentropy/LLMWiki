## Overview
Market regime is the backdrop against which all trading decisions are made. It encompasses growth/inflation dynamics, liquidity conditions, and volatility structure.

## Regime Components

### Growth/Inflation Regime
Three primary states:
- **Disinflationary Slowdown**: Growth declining, inflation falling
- **Reacceleration**: Growth and/or inflation picking up
- **Stagflation Risk**: Growth stalling, inflation persistent or rising

### Liquidity Backdrop
- **Fed Policy**: Rate path, QE/QT stance
- **Treasury Issuance**: Government financing needs
- **QT/QE Proxies**: Balance sheet flows and derivatives positioning
- **Dollar Liquidity**: Global funding conditions

### Volatility Regime
Three primary states:
- **Suppressed**: Low realized and implied volatility, complacency risk
- **Transitioning**: Volatility stepping up, models adapting
- **Unstable**: High and unpredictable volatility, momentum reversals

### Confidence Level
Assessment: High / Medium / Low

## Regime Shifts
Key to macro trading: identifying when regimes change before consensus does.

### Observable Confirmation Signals
- Curve inversion/flattening/steepening
- DXY breaks (currency trends)
- Credit spread widening/tightening
- Equity breadth deterioration/improvement
- Commodity spike/collapse
- VIX/GARCH regime changes
- Yield curve reconditioning

### Why It Matters
Regime drives expected returns, risks, and correlations:
- **Growth regime**: Cyclicals outperform, credit spreads widen in slowdown
- **Inflation regime**: Real assets outperform, duration risk matters
- **Liquidity regime**: Vol regime and crowded positioning create squeeze risk
- **Volatility regime**: Option value, gamma exposure, CTA flow mechanics change

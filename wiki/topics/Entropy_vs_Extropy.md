# Entropy vs Extropy

[[Curtis_Yarvin]]'s thermodynamic reframe of the left/right political axis. Left = entropy (decay, dissolution, leveling). Right = extropy (active construction of order, complexity, vitality). Reframes politics as a cosmic battle between decay and deliberate creation rather than policy granularity.

## In this wiki

Primary source is [[Curtis_Yarvin]]; the frame is used in [[Political_Philosophy]], [[Political_Commentary]], and [[Philosophy_and_Meaning]]. Applied loosely in [[Business_Philosophy]] and [[Masculinity_and_Men]] — anywhere deliberate structure resists decay.

## Key themes

- **Entropy (Left).** Natural tendency toward disorder. Deconstructs hierarchies, traditions, standards, borders, merit-based systems. Creates equality on paper but produces breakdown. "Things can just decay." Historical observation: "Cthulhu always swims left" ([[Curtis_Yarvin]]).
- **Extropy (Right).** Active increase in order, complexity, intelligence, vitality, capability. Life itself is extropic — builds organized structures (cells → organisms → societies). Strong hierarchies, sovereign decision-making, patchwork-style or corporate-sovereign governance that can actually act ([[Curtis_Yarvin]]).
- **Conservatism is slow-motion leftism.** Most "conservatives" are just slower versions of the entropic trend. Real right-wing energy is creative and order-building — not preservation, but construction ([[Curtis_Yarvin]]).
- **The reframe.** Politics = cosmic battle between decay and deliberate creation, not policy details. Left wants systems to rot; Right fights rot with active building ([[Philosophy_and_Meaning]]).
- **Distinctiveness as extropy.** Bezos' shareholder letter: "It takes continuous work to maintain distinctiveness. The world pulls at you in 1000 ways" — companies, like organisms, fight equilibrium decay ([[Business_Philosophy]]).
- **Sovereignty decay.** When governments cannot maintain property rights or economic coherence, sovereignty itself becomes distressed — entropy manifesting institutionally ([[Political_Commentary]]).

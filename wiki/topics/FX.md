## Overview
Foreign exchange is where macro regimes often show up first. Currency moves precede equity/bond moves.

## Key Components

### USD Broad (Trade-Weighted)
- **What it is**: US dollar strength against basket of trading partner currencies
- **Drivers**: Interest rate differentials, growth expectations, risk sentiment
- **Trend**: Strong dollar usually = risk-off / high US rates
- **Relationship**: DXY and other broad indices track this

### Key Crosses
- **EUR/USD**: Dollar-euro; sensitive to ECB vs. Fed rate differentials, Eurozone growth
- **GBP/USD**: Dollar-pound; sensitive to BoE vs. Fed, UK growth and Brexit dynamics
- **USD/JPY**: Dollar-yen; yen is safe-haven, carry trade barometer; moves can be sharp

### EM Currencies
- **First to stress**: EM currencies weaken first when risk-off (capital flight)
- **Stress signal**: EM currency weakness precedes EM equity weakness
- **Contagion risk**: If EM currencies breaking down = systemic stress
- **Flow indicator**: EM inflows/outflows visible in currency moves

## Macro Relationships

### Risk-On / Risk-Off
- **Risk-on**: USD weak (capital flows to higher-yielding EM), EM currencies strong
- **Risk-off**: USD strong (flight to safety), EM currencies weak

### Interest Rate Differentials
- **Fed > ECB**: EUR/USD weaker (carry trades unwind EUR longs)
- **Fed < ECB**: EUR/USD stronger (carry trades build EUR longs)
- **Moves ahead of rates**: Currency markets price in rate expectations quickly

### Real Rate Differentials
- **US real rates > global**: USD rallies (better real return)
- **US real rates < global**: USD weakens (real return unattractive)

## Trading Implications
- **Carry trades**: Borrowing low-rate currency (yen) to buy high-rate currency (AUD, NZD, EM)
- **Carry unwind**: When risk-off, carry trades reverse = sharp currency moves
- **Yen safety**: Yen rallies in stress; yen weakness = complacency
- **EM stress**: EM currency weakness = red flag for stress ahead

# Design Thinking

The wiki's philosophical layer on design — distinct from [[Design_and_Aesthetics]], which is the tools-and-tools-and-more-tools catalog. This page captures the principles about *how to think* about design and taste.

## In this wiki

Sparsely referenced. [[Thoughts]] is the main source; [[Design_and_Aesthetics]] cross-links here for the conceptual content it doesn't inline. The through-line: taste is character revelation, and good design requires tension.

## Key themes

- **Aesthetic as unconscious confession.** "Your aesthetic taste is a map of your inner world projected outward. What you're drawn to reveals which parts of your spirit are awake" ([[Thoughts]], [[Design_and_Aesthetics]]). Taste = character revelation.
- **Beauty needs tension.** Strong aesthetic lives on the edge of something uncomfortable. Safe beauty reads as stock photography; beauty with tension sticks ([[Design_and_Aesthetics]]'s "Wicked Game" note).
- **Taste > technical skill.** Getting good at design requires developing aesthetic sensitivity, not just technical ability. Study Ralph Lauren, Tadao Ando, motion studios, typography ([[Design_and_Aesthetics]]).
- **Elon's blind spot.** Cited in [[Thoughts]] — bad taste, cringe logos, bad jokes. Doesn't understand the political utility of image. Aesthetic failure as strategic vulnerability.
- **Component thinking.** Break designs into smallest reusable units. Scalable systems beat one-off designs ([[Design_and_Aesthetics]]).
- **Intentional motion.** Movement, animation, and micro-interactions should feel intentional, not decorative. Design-engineering craft ≠ animation flourish ([[Design_and_Aesthetics]]).
- **Narrative packaging.** "Storytelling is the ultimate compression algorithm for human attention. Get narrative wrong, idea dies. Get it right, you bend steel" — design as narrative vehicle ([[Thoughts]], [[Philosophy_and_Meaning]]).

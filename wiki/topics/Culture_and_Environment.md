# Culture and Environment

The thesis that place — country, city, routine, physical surroundings — is a first-order cause of personality, more load-bearing than job title or life plan. Geography reshapes the person, whether they notice or not.

## In this wiki

Introduced in [[Culture]] and extended in [[Philosophy_and_Meaning]] under "Identity & Geographic Destiny." Sits underneath many of the dating, politics, and self-improvement observations — the implicit lever being: change your location, change your defaults.

## Key themes

- **Every country has an energy.** "People move to Japan and become minimal. People move to Mexico and their entire relationship with time softens. People move to New York and suddenly they can't sit still" (from [[Culture]]).
- **Personality is malleable.** Far more so than assumed. New surroundings = new defaults, pace, habits, values — absorbed through proximity, not effort (from [[X-Bookmarks]], [[Philosophy_and_Meaning]]).
- **Average of 5 places, not 5 people.** "You're the average of the 5 places, the 5 routines, and the 5 inputs you're exposed to most. Your commute shapes you. The weather shapes you. Every space you occupy is voting on who you become" ([[X-Bookmarks]]).
- **Place > job title.** Choosing where you live is one of the most important decisions — more than career choices ([[Culture]], [[Philosophy_and_Meaning]]).
- **Nature → boundaries.** "People who spend time in nature don't support mass immigration. Urbanites used to living like rats see no problem stuffing more in" ([[Political_Commentary]]) — environment shapes political values.
- **Climate enables civilization.** AC as one of the "signal inventions of history" for enabling development in tropical zones ([[Lee_Kuan_Yew]], [[Culture]]).

# Decision-Making

How the wiki thinks about choice under uncertainty — particularly the separation of decision quality from outcome quality, and the conditions under which intuitive judgment works or breaks down.

## In this wiki

Surfaces across [[Trading_Psychology]], [[Psychology]], and [[Thoughts]]. The recurring frame: good process in a noisy environment looks like bad process in short samples, and the discipline is not letting outcomes retrain process.

## Key themes

- **Decouple decision from outcome.** Market outcome ≠ quality of decision. Randomness masks poor reasoning; luck masks bad strategy. Only long-term separates signal from noise (from [[Trading_Psychology]]).
- **Intuitive vs. measured thinkers.** Intuitive decision-makers thrive when plugged in and information-saturated; they lose edge as information flow thins with age. Measured thinkers are less spectacular but safer ([[Psychology]]). See [[Genius_and_Madness]].
- **Overthinking = underacting.** "All miserable people overthink and underact." System: just act. Imperfect action beats perfect planning ([[Psychology]]).
- **High-agency as trait.** "Single most important trait in friends, coworkers, partners, kids: high agency" (from [[Thoughts]]).
- **Variance tolerance.** Most traders misread losing streaks as strategy failure rather than noise. Decouple process from outcome ([[0xaporia]] in [[CT]]).
- **Physiognomy & feedback.** Repeated decisions reshape the actor: aggressive masculine decisions change features, frame, muscle ease ([[Thoughts]]).

# Neurodiversity

Umbrella for cognitive variation — ADHD, autism, and adjacent neurotypes — treated in the wiki less as a diagnostic category and more as a cognitive-style distinction with social implications.

## In this wiki

Introduced as a related concept for [[ADHD]]. Extended through scattered observations in [[Psychology]] and [[Thoughts]] that frame neurodivergent cognition as structurally different — less consensus-filtered, more object-truth-oriented.

## Key themes

- **Free-thinking advantage.** Neurodivergent thinkers (autism/ADHD) parse information objectively ("is this true?") rather than through social safety filter ("will others be OK with me thinking this?") — from [[Psychology]], [[ADHD]].
- **Republic of free thinkers.** "Only high-T alpha males and neurodivergent people (autists) are actually free to parse information objectively" ([[Psychology]]).
- **Autism ↔ integrity.** "The correlation between autism and traits like honor and integrity is astronomically high relative to general population. Autists don't do social theater; they do truth" (from [[Thoughts]]).
- **ADHD friendship mechanics.** No friendship degradation — can pick up after months or years of silence. Positive social trait that cuts against stereotypes ([[ADHD]], [[Psychology]]).
- **ADHD as circadian.** Reconceptualization as a circadian rhythm disorder rather than pure attention deficit ([[Medical]], [[Circadian_Rhythm]]).
- **Cognitive-style asymmetry.** Neurodivergents act as anomaly detectors where consensus-filtered cognition fails — socially costly, epistemically valuable.

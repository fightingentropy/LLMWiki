# Japanese Philosophy & Cultural Concepts

Landing page for the cluster of Japanese cultural ideas the human endorsed via `@AlpacaAurelius` in [[Go Do]] — "Japan gets it… cannot wait to visit." Short factual definitions; cross-linked to the existing wiki where the concept already has a home.

## Core Concepts

### Ikigai
"Reason for being." The intersection of what you love, what you're good at, what the world needs, and what you can be paid for. Used as a life-purpose framework. Ties to [[Philosophy_and_Meaning]] (meaning as direction, not hedonic target) and [[Self_Improvement]] (high agency, one-thing-per-day).

### Wabi-Sabi
Aesthetic centred on impermanence and imperfection — the beauty of the weathered, asymmetric, incomplete. Antidote to sterile optimization. Connects to [[Design_and_Aesthetics]] and the "beauty / toxicity" tension in [[Youtube]].

### Kaizen
Continuous improvement through small, compounding changes. Originated in post-war Japanese manufacturing (Toyota); exported globally. Directly mirrors the "compounding delta" principle in [[Self_Improvement]] — tiny +1s beat occasional heroics.

### Kintsugi
The craft of repairing broken pottery with gold lacquer, highlighting the fracture rather than hiding it. Damage becomes part of the object's value. A visual metaphor for scars-as-identity; relates to the shame/darkness framing in [[Self_Improvement]] (fight darkness, but carry it visibly rather than pretending it isn't there).

### Omotenashi
Hospitality as selflessness — anticipating the guest's needs before they're voiced, without expectation of reward. Service ethos beyond transaction.

### Onsen
Hot springs — communal bathing in natural geothermal water. Cultural institution, not just leisure. On the human's list from [[Go Do]] to experience directly.

### Forest Bathing (Shinrin-yoku)
Deliberate time spent in forest environments. Officially recognised preventative health practice in Japan. Parallel to circadian/outdoor-time arguments in [[Body_Optimization]] and [[Mental_Health]].

### Tea Ceremony (Chadō / Sadō)
Ritualised tea preparation. The point is the attention, sequence, and presence — not the drink. Instance of ritual-as-contemplative-practice.

### Wagyu
Japanese beef, genetically and husbandry-distinct (extensive marbling, long finishing). Craft-food reference point.

### Tatami
Woven-reed flooring modules sized to standard proportions; the floor becomes architectural and dimensional unit. Instance of material-as-system.

### Kimono
Traditional garment; preserved as formal and ceremonial wear. Instance of living craft tradition.

### Raw Food (Sushi / Sashimi)
Culinary tradition centred on ingredient integrity and knife craft. Minimal intervention as respect for material.

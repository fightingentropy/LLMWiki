# Self-Improvement

## The Compounding Delta Principle
"Power of replacing tiny bad decisions (-1, -3) with tiny good ones (+1, +2). Life = accumulation of micro-choices, not catastrophic blowups."

Example: Basketball—miss wide-open layup, get frustrated, give up open 3. Score changes -3 but expected value was +2, so actual swing is -5. String a few of those together and you're down -40 (like Suns down massive to Luka).

**Most failure is slow erosion, not single blow.** Most success is steady accumulation of +1s.

## Imperfect Action > Perfect Planning
"All miserable people: overthink and underact."

System: **Aim, fire, correct later.** Imperfect action >> perfect planning.

Preparatory state (research, optimization, system-design) is comfortable procrastination. Execution is where reality hits.

**Source flagged for study** (via [[Go Do]]): [[Alex_Hormozi]] on [[Chris_Williamson]]'s podcast, [timestamp](https://youtu.be/Gk8EGWoGnEQ?t=3590) — same video that produced the "do epic shit" / "only way is through" bookmarks in [[Youtube]]. Hormozi's version: perfect plans are procrastination; the plan corrects against contact with reality, not against more planning.

Compounds with **kaizen** ([[Japanese_Philosophy]]): continuous small corrections in flight, not a single perfect launch.

## One Thing Per Day
"After accounting for work, relationships, obligations, I have capacity for one optional thing daily. I'm at peace with that. Better than burning out trying to do 3-4 things."

**Acceptance prevents revenge procrastination, stress, and self-judgment.**

## High Agency = Core Attribute
"Single most important trait in friends, coworkers, partners, kids: high agency."

Agency = ability to make things happen, solve problems, move forward despite obstacles. Everything else is subordinate.

## Shame as Fuel vs. Parasite
"Darkness you carry inside is lying. It's tricked you into believing it's a superpower. Shame creates mirage that its existence is who you are."

Shame burns painful memories into mind (preventing mistakes), but it's **parasitic not symbiotic.** It steals presence and energy that could build flourishing existence.

**Fight darkness, be kind to self, keep moving forward.**

## Embarrassment as Entry Cost
"Embarrassment is cost of entry. If you aren't willing to look like foolish beginner, you'll never become graceful master."

Mastery requires public incompetence first.

## The Enthusiasm Gap
"You're getting lapped by people 50% dumber than you because they don't overthink. They aim, fire, correct later."

Smart people paralyzed by possibility space; average people just move.

## Self-Labeling & Reality
"Words are like spells. Be careful labeling yourself 'traumatized' or 'depressed'—they start becoming real."

Language rewires neurology. Self-conception becomes self-fulfilling prophecy.

## Reading as Emotional Systematization
"Reading is systematizing the way you feel."

Converting emotional experience into language structure creates understanding and perspective.

## Intentional Versus Accidental Optimization
Hyper-optimization culture (morning routines, productivity systems, biohacking) can mask avoidance. **Preparatory state replaces executory state.**

True optimization: doing the thing, not optimizing how you'll do the thing.

## The Urgency of Serendipity
"If big publisher/dealer/executive suggests appointment, cancel anything planned. You may never see window again."

**Opportunities don't grow on trees.** Optionality value of high-trust encounters with important people = immense.

Living in big cities invaluable because you increase odds of serendipitous encounters. Envelope of possibility contains 10x more for those positioned in it.

## Delusion as Tool

Bookmarked via [[Youtube]]: "Why delusion is important." Functional self-belief that runs ahead of the evidence is a feature, not a bug — it gets you to act before the data would justify it, and the action itself produces the data. Related to [[Trading_Psychology]]: intuitive operators who commit before consensus are the ones who catch the move.

Delusion is not the opposite of realism here. It's realism about what belief does, not just what belief claims.

## Motivation as Reframe

"How can you lack motivation?" (bookmarked via [[Youtube]]) — treats absent motivation as a category error rather than a deficiency to be fixed. The reframe: motivation follows action, not the reverse. See also [[Mental_Health]] on overthinking paralysis.

## Autonomy & Self-Direction
"The state can substitute for what women want from men, but can't substitute for what men want from women."

Humans need genuine autonomy, purpose, agency. Systems can provide comfort, not meaning.

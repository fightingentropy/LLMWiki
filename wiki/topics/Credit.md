## Overview
Credit is where tail risk often emerges. Credit markets are less liquid and can dislocate sharply in stress.

## Key Credit Metrics

### Credit Spreads
- **IG spreads**: Investment-grade corporate bonds vs. treasuries; 100-200bps is normal
- **HY spreads**: High-yield (junk) bonds; 400-600bps is normal
- **Spread widening**: Risk-off = credit spreads widen (flight to safety, default fears)
- **Spread tightening**: Risk-on = spreads tighten (complacency, risk appetite)

### Specific Credit Events
- **Refinancing stress**: Maturing debt rolling over at higher rates
- **Covenant breaches**: Companies failing to meet credit agreement terms
- **Rating downgrade**: Signals deteriorating credit quality
- **Default**: Company unable to pay; holder loses value

### Credit Market Indicators
- **High-yield spreads > 600bps**: Stress signal; some defaults likely
- **IG spreads > 200bps**: Early warning; not extreme but elevated
- **Credit default swap spreads**: Derivative pricing of default risk
- **Lending standards**: Banks tightening credit = economy weakening

## Credit Cycle Dynamics

### Boom Phase
- Low spreads, strong earnings, easy refinancing
- New issues priced easily
- Leveraged buyouts and acquisitions
- Risk: Leverage builds without recognition

### Peak Phase
- Spreads compress to lows
- Earnings growth slowing but valuations highest
- Refinancing happening at "best" rates
- Risk: Vulnerable if anything breaks

### Bust Phase
- Earnings miss expectations
- Default forecasts rising
- Refinancing becomes difficult/expensive
- Spreads widen; credit crunch develops

### Trough Phase
- Spreads wide, values attractive
- Defaults occurring
- Fear at extremes
- Opportunity: Credit recovers

## Credit-Equity Divergence
When equities hold up but spreads widen = warning sign:
- Market is repricing default risk
- Equities haven't caught up to credit repricing
- Equity correction likely ahead

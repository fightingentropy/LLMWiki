## Core Principles

### Truth vs. Perception
**"Truth doesn't necessarily move markets. Perception & Uncertainty/Certainty transitions do."**

Markets reward those who understand what other market participants believe, not what is objectively true. Transitions from uncertainty to certainty (or vice versa) create the biggest moves.

### The Prison of Trading
From "The Prison Of Financial Mediocrity":
> "It is foolish to think that you can withdraw from the Exchange after you have tasted the sweetness of the honey. He who has entered the charmed circle of the Exchange is in eternal agitation and sits in a prison, the key of which lies in the ocean and the bars of which are never opened."

Once you enter trading, it consumes you. This is both warning and reality: understand the commitment required.

### Resilience Through Adversity
From the story of [[Toto Wolff]]:
> "The world breaks everyone, and afterward, many are stronger at the broken places."

Trading breaks traders. The question is whether you emerge stronger at the broken places. This applies to both individual trades and psychological resilience.

## Key Practices

### Regime-First Thinking
- Determine market regime before position-taking
- Let regime assessment drive position sizing
- Expect regime shifts to invalidate prior convictions

### Signal Over Narrative
- Focus on what is happening, not why it's happening
- Observable confirmations matter more than predictions
- Narratives are useful for crowd coordination, not price discovery

### Positioning for Extremes
- Identify positioning extremes (consensus trades, squeeze risk)
- Trade mean reversion when positioning extreme
- Trade momentum when regime is clear and consensus uncertain

### Specific, Observable Triggers
- Establish what would confirm or invalidate your view
- Set specific levels and events as decision points
- Avoid narrative-driven indefinite holding

## Capital Allocation Asymmetry (from [[CT]])
Attributed to cobie's coach: "you act too quickly when you are trying to grow capital, and act too slowly when you are trying to protect capital." Most account destruction comes from this asymmetry — eager to size up, hesitant to cut.

## Sit on Your Ass (from [[CT]])
Lord Ashdrake / [[cobie]]:
> "What Traders should be like: 'Sit on my ass, then a few times a year make massive bank then sit on my ass again'"

Directly contradicts [[GiganticRebirth]]'s "sacrifice your weekends" framing — both are in [[CT]]; which applies depends on regime richness.

## Decouple Process from Outcome (from [[CT]])
[[0xaporia]] on variance: losing streaks of 7+ happen 32-69% of the time in 50-40% win-rate strategies over 100 trades. Traders who flee the nonlinear grind for new strategies are interpreting noise as signal.

## Orderflow vs Newsflow (from [[CT]])
[[HsakaTrades]]: "The orderflow frontruns the newsflow." Price moves first; the narrative comes to justify it. See also [[Trading_Psychology]] on post-hoc narratives.

## Poker Discipline (from [[CT]])
[[0xaporia]]: poker players know variance is the game; novice traders conflate constant activity with progress. Restraint — not playing every hand — is a structural edge.

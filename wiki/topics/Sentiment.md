## Overview
Market sentiment is the aggregate emotion and conviction of participants. Extremes in sentiment often coincide with market inflections.

## Sentiment Indicators

### Crypto Sentiment (CT)
- **Crypto Twitter sentiment**: Collective view on direction and catalysts
- **Narrative dominance**: Which story is winning (bull case vs. bear case)
- **Influencer positioning**: What are popular traders saying
- **Retail positioning**: Follower count and engagement = retail conviction

### Positioning Extremes
When many participants are on the same side:
- **Consensus long**: Everyone owns the asset = no buyers left = vulnerable to pullbacks
- **Consensus short**: Everyone is short = squeeze risk if wrong
- **No consensus**: Inflection period = uncertain markets

### Sentiment Reversals
Market reversals often occur when:
- **Bullish extreme**: Headlines most optimistic = final push up then reversal down
- **Bearish extreme**: Headlines most pessimistic = capitulation, reversal up
- **Narrative shift**: Story changes (rate cut => rate hike) = sentiment whipsaws

## Measurement
Sentiment is subjective and hard to quantify, but proxies include:
- **Put/call ratios**: Options sentiment
- **Surveys**: Investor Intelligence, AAII
- **Social media**: Twitter sentiment, Reddit positioning
- **Positioning data**: Commitment of Traders (COT) reports
- **Extremes**: When everyone agrees, look for reversal

## Cautions
- **Self-fulfilling**: Sentiment-driven moves don't need fundamentals to justify them
- **Tricky timing**: Sentiment can stay extreme for longer than expected
- **Narrative trap**: Hot narrative can keep you on wrong side
- **Reversal vs. continuation**: Sentiment extreme doesn't guarantee reversal; confirm with technicals/regime

# GS38

**HSE Guidance Note GS38 — "Electrical test equipment for use on low voltage electrical systems"**. Non-statutory guidance from the Health and Safety Executive covering the safe design and condition of test leads, probes, clips, and voltage indicators used by electrical workers.

It is the practical reference for what a "safe" lead set looks like — and it is the standard cited throughout safe isolation and live testing procedures.

## Why GS38 Matters

Test leads and probes are what the tester actually touches while working at or near live parts. Their condition is directly linked to personal safety. The source notes make the point explicitly:

*"Why important: Test leads and probes are what the tester handles at potentially live points — their condition is critical to personal safety."*

GS38 translates that risk into design and condition requirements.

## Key Requirements

### Probe Design

- **Finger guards** (shrouds / barriers) on probes to keep fingers clear of the metal tip under fault or slip conditions.
- **Exposed probe tip length ≤ 4 mm.** The originally recommended exposure was up to about 19 mm; current practice (and most modern instruments) is 4 mm or less, with many being spring-retracted.
- **Insulated probe bodies** rated for the circuit being tested.
- **Spring-loaded / retractable tips** preferred where practical.

### Leads

- **Robust, flexible insulation** with no cuts, abrasions, or visible conductor.
- **Adequate length** for the task but not excessive (to reduce trip/snag risk).
- **Colour-coded** where sensible (red/black, or per tester standard) to prevent mis-connection.
- **Securely terminated** at both the instrument end and the probe end.
- **Fused leads** or built-in **current-limiting resistors** where the instrument design requires them — this limits the energy released in the event of a short between probes across live conductors.

### Connectors and Clips

- **Shrouded** 4 mm plugs on the instrument end.
- **Crocodile clips** used for hands-free work must themselves have insulated jaws with minimal exposed metal.

### Inspection Before Use

GS38 expects that leads and probes are visually checked **before every use**:

- Full length of lead inspected for damage
- Probe tips intact and retraction (if fitted) working freely
- Connections at both ends secure
- No loose copper strands
- Continuity of leads confirmed if in doubt

Damaged leads are replaced — not taped or field-repaired. Replacements should themselves be GS38 compliant.

## Role in Safe Isolation

[[Safe Isolation]] depends on a known-good voltage indicator and leads. The standard GS38-aligned sequence is:

1. **Prove** the voltage indicator on a known live source or proving unit.
2. **Use** the indicator to prove dead on the load side of the isolation point, in a consistent sequence (earth→line, earth→neutral, line→neutral).
3. **Re-prove** the indicator on a known live source afterwards — confirms it was still working during step 2.

A voltage indicator whose leads are not GS38 compliant undermines the whole procedure.

## Relationship to Other Standards

- [[BS EN 61010]] — safety of the test instrument itself (construction, insulation, CAT ratings).
- [[BS EN 61557]] — performance/accuracy of installation test instruments.
- GS38 — the leads and probes you attach to those instruments.

All three are cited together in the source notes: *"Test equipment should meet standards covering safety and accuracy: 61010 (safety), 61557 (accuracy), GS38 (HSE guidance on safe use and condition of leads and probes)."*

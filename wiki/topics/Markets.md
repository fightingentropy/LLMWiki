# Markets

## Fundamental Truths

**Markets are 6-8 months ahead of news.** Price action precedes explanation. What seems like prediction is actually: price moved already, news follows later.

**News truthfulness is immaterial.** Market participants fixate on whether headlines are true/false, but actual veracity doesn't move price. **It's market's reaction to news and duration of that reaction that's informative.**

## Market Seasonality

- **January → October:** Strong trading activity
- **Late August → December:** Where most annual moves happen
- **Week after July 4:** Absolute nadir of trading volume
- **September:** Year actually begins (post-summer hiatus); FOMC meetings cause wild gyrations
- **May-August:** "Sell in May and Go Away" period; low volume and vulnerability
- **Day after Thanksgiving → Christmas:** Lull before year-end
- **Late December → Jan 1:** Extremely low activity until new year

**Why seasonality matters:** Low volume can trigger unexpected movements not following typical patterns. Placid waters pierced by sudden current.

## Reflexive Market Dynamics

Reflexive pumps don't start with buyers; they start with **no one left willing to sell.**

Sequence:
1. Stubborn holder base refuses to let go
2. Synthetic supply shock created
3. Aggressive shorts pile in
4. Disbelief rally erupts (sideline capital waits for dip that never comes)
5. FOMO kicks in; price chases into thin air
6. Zero marginal sellers + infinite shorts to cover = parabolic moves
7. Attention games dominate

## Equity Valuation Decomposition

- **Book Value** = What's real
- **Discounted Earnings (3yr)** = What's likely
- **Everything else** = Hopes & Dreams

"Long way to go before Hopes & Dreams turn into Despair & Nightmares."

## Bear Market Structure (Rhea, 1935)

Bears structured in three phases:
1. **Abandonment of hopes** - Bull market's foundation crumbles
2. **Reduced earning power** - Dividends fall
3. **Distress liquidation** - Must-sell securities for living expenses

Each phase separated by secondary reaction (bear market rally), often mistaken for bull start.

## Women & Consumer Spending

Women control/influence **85% of consumer spending** in US (~$31.8 trillion globally). Economy propped up by female consumption patterns.

## Perpetual Futures Product-Market Fit

Best PMF in crypto. Robert Shiller (1992) proposed perpetuals for illiquid assets (homes, labor costs, CPI). Creates derivatives markets for hard-to-price assets where traditional markets don't exist.

## Supply Shocks & Pricing

"You can make things cheap and it fixes lots of problems. But weird things happen at $0.00."

When price approaches zero (free healthcare, free college):
- Demand explodes beyond rational bounds
- System collapses under load
- Incentive structures break

Example: NHS (free healthcare) = 9mo-1yr waiting lists, 7M people waiting. System crumbles.

## Tokenization & Market Structure

Current reality: Public/private securities held in segregated databases managed by banks, brokers, law firms. Massive inefficiency, fees, errors.

Tokenization enables:
- 24/7/365 global trading (vs. market hours)
- Seamless collateralization and lending
- Non-custodial protocols
- Broader investor access
- Ability to send assets globally instantly

**Most benefit:** Assets without robust existing markets (real estate, IP, startup shares).

## Overview
The US Dollar Index (DXY) is a trade-weighted measure of US dollar strength against major currency baskets. It's a critical macro indicator.

## What Is DXY?
- **Composition**: Weighted basket of major currency pairs (EUR 57%, JPY 13%, GBP 12%, CAD 9%, SEK 4%, CHF 3%)
- **Calculation**: 100 × geometric average of rate changes
- **Range**: Typically 90-110; extremes rare

## DXY Relationships

### Macro Implications
- **Strong DXY (>105)**: Risk-off, high US rates, global stress
- **Weak DXY (<95)**: Risk-on, low US rates, growth-positive
- **DXY at extremes**: Often precedes reversals (reverting to mean 100)

### Cross-Asset Effects
- **Commodities**: Inverse relationship (weak dollar = expensive commodities, all denominated in USD)
- **Gold/Silver**: Inverse relationship; weak dollar makes gold cheaper globally, attractive
- **EM currencies**: Weak dollar = EM strength; strong dollar = EM weakness
- **Equity earnings**: Strong dollar = weaker international earnings when translated to USD

### Interest Rate Sensitivity
- **Rising US rates > global rates**: DXY strengthens (higher real return)
- **Falling US rates < global rates**: DXY weakens (lower relative return)

## Trading Implications
- **Dollar strength plays**: Strong DXY good for: USD borrowers, US exporters in real terms, US rates relative to global
- **Dollar weakness plays**: Weak DXY good for: commodities, EM equities, precious metals, importers
- **Carry trades**: Weak dollar enables carry trades (borrow cheap USD, invest globally)
- **Carry unwind**: Strong dollar forces unwinding of carry (yen carry especially brutal)

## Levels & Technicals
- **100 level**: Psychological anchor and mean
- **105 level**: Extreme strength; rarely sustained
- **95 level**: Extreme weakness; rare
- **Support/resistance**: Previous highs/lows create levels

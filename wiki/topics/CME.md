## Overview
Chicago Mercantile Exchange - the world's largest centralized derivatives exchange. Primary venue for commodity, equity, and currency futures trading.

## Key Products
- **Commodities**: Oil, natural gas, gold, silver, agricultural
- **Equities**: S&P 500, Nasdaq, Russell 2000 futures
- **Currencies**: Dollar index, FX futures
- **Rates**: Treasury futures, Eurodollar

## Significance for Markets
- **Price discovery**: CME is primary venue for commodity and equity index futures
- **Leverage**: Enables institutional and retail leverage
- **Global access**: Available to traders worldwide
- **Regulation**: CFTC regulated; transparent position reporting

## Microstructure
- **Centralized matching**: Central order book
- **Pit trading legacy**: Now electronic
- **Liquidity**: Generally deep, especially for major contracts
- **Hours**: Extended hours (24/5 for most products)

## Competitive Landscape
Increasingly competing with:
- [[Hyperliquid]] in crypto/decentralized
- Crypto perps (better accessibility)
- Offshore exchanges (regulatory arbitrage)

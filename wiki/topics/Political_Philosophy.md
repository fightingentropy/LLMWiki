# Political Philosophy

Foundational-level political thinking — not policy, but the underlying frames the wiki uses to organize political questions. Dominant frame is [[Curtis_Yarvin]]'s entropy-vs-extropy reset of the left/right axis.

## In this wiki

Primary anchor is [[Curtis_Yarvin]]. Extended through [[Philosophy_and_Meaning]] and applied in [[Political_Commentary]]. The move: treat politics as cosmic/thermodynamic rather than policy-granular.

## Key themes

- **Entropy vs. Extropy.** Yarvin's reframe: Left = entropy (deconstructs hierarchies, traditions, standards; equality on paper → breakdown in practice). Right = extropy (actively builds order, complexity, vitality; strong hierarchies, sovereign decision-making, patchwork/corporate-sovereign models). See [[Entropy_vs_Extropy]].
- **Conservative vs. liberal misses the point.** Most "conservatives" are just slower versions of the entropic trend. Real right-wing energy is creative and order-building, not slow-motion leftism ([[Curtis_Yarvin]]).
- **Sovereignty as distressed asset.** When governments cannot maintain basic property rights, wage justice, or economic coherence, sovereignty itself becomes distressed — invites capital flight, brain drain, institutional capture ([[Political_Commentary]]).
- **Nominal vs. functional system labels.** UK nominally capitalist but functionally communist (extreme wage compression); China nominally communist but functionally capitalist. Follow incentives and outcomes, not rhetoric ([[Political_Commentary]]).
- **Geographic determinism.** Environment votes on political values — nature dwellers don't support mass immigration; urbanites normalize radical openness ([[Political_Commentary]]).
- **"Cthulhu always swims left."** Historical-drift observation attributed to Yarvin — systems decay toward entropy absent deliberate counterforce.

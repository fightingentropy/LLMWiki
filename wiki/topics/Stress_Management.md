# Stress Management

How the wiki frames stress: not as a single thing to minimise but as two opposite things to balance. Acute stress is fuel; chronic stress is poison.

## In this wiki

Anchored in [[Body_Optimization]] as a core distinction; the prescription is symmetric — eliminate chronic stressors, deliberately reintroduce acute ones. Modernity is treated as a pathological distribution: too much chronic, too little acute.

## Key themes

- **Acute stress is beneficial.** Competition, high-intensity exercise, short conflict make you better, stronger, more adaptive (from [[Body_Optimization]], via [@NoahRyanCo](https://x.com/NoahRyanCo/status/1928925779699839308)).
- **Chronic stress is toxic.** Social isolation, bad sleep, soulless work, 24/7 news cycles produce burnout and depression.
- **The modern problem.** Abundant chronic stress, void of acute stress. Prescription: eliminate chronic; deliberately reintroduce acute.
- **Boxed breathing.** Named stress-regulation and focus tool ([[Body_Optimization]]).
- **Cold exposure and cold showers.** Immediate trajectory-setting effects comparable to medication ([[Body_Optimization]]).
- **Movement as medicine.** Walking, dancing, sun exposure, creating — all therapeutic ([[Body_Optimization]]).
- **Antifragility frame.** Growth (personal, economic, biological) often accelerates under stress, not peace — sought variability, not comfort ([[Quotes]], Taleb-adjacent).

# Economics

Lightweight stub. The wiki doesn't develop economics as its own discipline — the economic content is embedded inside [[Markets]], [[Business_Strategy]], and [[Political_Commentary]]. This page collects the cross-cutting economic observations that don't fit cleanly into any of those.

## In this wiki

Mostly price-theory and incentive observations from [[Bizz_and_Finance]] and [[Culture]], plus the UK wage-compression analysis in [[Political_Commentary]]. For macro/trading-specific economics see [[Macro Trading]], [[Rates]], [[Credit]].

## Key themes

- **Price → $0 breaks incentives.** "You can make things cheap and it fixes lots of problems. But weird things happen at $0.00." Free healthcare and free college cases in [[Bizz_and_Finance]] — marginal cost near zero destroys the scarcity signal and breaks the system.
- **Consumer spending skews female.** Women control or influence ~85% of US consumer spending (~$31.8T globally). Economy propped up by female consumption patterns ([[Bizz_and_Finance]]).
- **Nominal vs. functional labels.** UK nominally capitalist but functionally communist via wage compression (3.04:1 vs. USSR's 5:1 floor); China nominally communist but functionally capitalist ([[Political_Commentary]]). Follow incentives and outcomes, not rhetoric.
- **Managed competition as policy tool.** China's [[Catfish_Effect]] introduces curated outside pressure to break domestic stagnation — a macro-level intervention without Western disruptive-capitalism framing.
- **Tokenization of illiquid assets.** Tokenization and perpetual futures (Shiller 1992) create market structure for assets without one — real estate, IP, startup shares ([[Bizz_and_Finance]]).
- **Equity valuation stack.** Book value = real; 3yr discounted earnings = likely; everything else = Hopes & Dreams ([[Markets]]).

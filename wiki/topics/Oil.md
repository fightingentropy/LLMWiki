## Overview
Oil is fundamentally a cyclical commodity driven by supply and demand dynamics, with significant geopolitical tail risk.

## Key Drivers

### Fundamentals
- **OPEC Production**: OPEC quotas and actual output, strategic release decisions
- **Non-OPEC Supply**: US shale, Russia, other producers
- **Global Demand**: GDP growth, transportation, manufacturing activity
- **Geopolitical Events**: Wars, sanctions, supply disruptions (Strait of Hormuz, pipelines, etc.)

### Policy & Technology
- **Energy Policy**: Support for EVs, renewable energy investment
- **Renewable Transition**: Long-term demand pressure from EV adoption
- **Technology**: Shale revolution changed structural supply dynamics

### Cycle Mechanics
1. **High prices**: Incentivize exploration and production
2. **Supply ramp**: New projects come online, oversupply builds
3. **Price crash**: Excess supply kills exploration/production spending
4. **Supply gap**: Underinvestment creates structural shortage
5. **Price spike**: Return to step 1

## Trading Implications
- **Cyclical**: Correlated with growth; rallies in risk-on, falls in risk-off
- **Geopolitical**: Supply disruptions can spike price regardless of fundamentals
- **Tail risk**: Geopolitical shock could spike oil 50%+ in days
- **Policy sensitive**: Energy policy changes are slow but structural

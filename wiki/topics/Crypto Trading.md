## Cycle Philosophy (from [[CT]])

### Supercycle vs inverse supercycle
- [[cobie]]: crypto as a decade-long supercycle bubble — "siren for the market top of the multi-decade Everything Bubble" (from [[CT]]).
- [[GCRClassic]]: inverse supercycle is crypto going to 0 as gen-z prefers memes to growth stocks and UBI/joblessness create "generation moonshot" (from [[CT]]).

Both takes rely on the same demographic observation — desperate, lonely, lottery-ticket-chasing youth — but reach opposite price conclusions. [[DegenSpartan]] explicitly contrasts "ingenuity/self-sovereignty" vs "degradation/greed" framings of the same asset class.

### Dilution kills bulls
[[Pentosh1]]: "The end simply comes when the dilution exceeds the amount of capital inflows" (from [[CT]]).

### Low-conviction money late
[[cobie]]: boomer LPs bid "new projects" at cycle tops because they perceive low entry prices as low risk — the foundation of the "outerspace metaverse hotel" fundraise (from [[CT]]).

### PvP vs PvE
[[Rewkang]]: crypto cycles alternate between PvP (trader-dominant) and PvE (retail-paycheck-DCA) regimes. Mean-reversion heuristics that work in PvP break in PvE (from [[CT]]).

### Cascades as secondary effect
@fewseethis in [[CT]]: "cascades occur as a RESULT of prices going down within a levered environment. in other words a cascade is a secondary effect." See [[Liquidations]], [[Leverage]].

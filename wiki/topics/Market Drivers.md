## Overview
Market drivers are the forces that move prices. Understanding which drivers are active in each regime is critical to trading.

## Key Drivers by Category

### Sentiment & Flow
- **CT Sentiment**: Community sentiment and positioning (crypto)
- **Stablecoin Dominance**: USDT/USDC flows indicate commitment to/from risk
- **Stablecoin Yields**: Borrow/lend rates reveal leverage and funding pressure
- **Funding Rates**: Perpetual futures funding extremes indicate crowding

### Technical & Mechanical
- **Moving Averages**: Momentum confirmation and trend structure
- **Liquidations**: Cascading liquidations drive volatility spikes
- **Options Positioning**: Gamma exposure and vega dynamics
- **Market Structure**: Bid-ask spread, order flow imbalances

### Fundamental & Macro
- **Macro Regime**: Growth, inflation, liquidity backdrop
- **Catalysts**: Events with binary outcomes (earnings, policy, geopolitical)
- **Top Trader Positioning**: Notable players' conviction levels

### Mechanical Flows
- **CTA/Systematic Flow**: Trend-following and risk parity rebalances
- **Dealer Gamma**: Hedging flows from option sales
- **Redemptions**: ETF/fund outflows creating mechanical selling

## Hierarchy of Drivers
Not all drivers matter equally in all regimes:
- In **trending markets**: momentum and flow dominate
- In **choppy markets**: technicals and gamma are whip-saws
- In **regime transitions**: positioning extremes matter most
- In **macro events**: fundamental catalysts dominate

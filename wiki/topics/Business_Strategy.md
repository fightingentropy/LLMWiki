# Business Strategy

## The Catfish Effect (鲶鱼效应)
Chinese business strategy concept: introduce managed competition to activate innovation.

**Origin:** Norwegian sardine fishing—fishermen transported sardines in tanks; many died from inactivity. Adding catfish kept sardines active and more survived. 

**Application:** Rather than destructive market disruption (Western model), introduce "stress on purpose because stagnation is bigger risk."

**Case study - Tesla in China:**
- Government frustrated with domestic EV sector (fraud, half-baked companies cashing subsidies)
- Gave Tesla rare approvals: full ownership, fast-tracked factory deals, land, logistics
- Created intentional competitive pressure: "Hey Chinese companies, swim or die. Catfish coming."
- Result: Pace of innovation accelerated across industry (operations, design, UX)

**Key difference from West:** Competition curated, introduced, welcomed—not to destroy domestic players but to get them moving. This requires trust and long-term thinking absent in quarterly capitalism.

## Consumer Spending & Gender
Women control/influence 85% of US consumer spending (~$31.8 trillion globally). Economy fundamentally propped up by female consumption patterns.

Implication: Understanding female consumer psychology = understanding economic structure.

Company fundamentals matter more than entry price, but entry price determines margin of safety.

## Distinctiveness as Competitive Moat
"World wants you typical. Demands continuous work to maintain distinctiveness."

From Bezos' final shareholder letter: Like biological systems resisting entropy, companies resist pressure to merge into equilibrium with environment.

Amazon's challenge: Stay distinctive despite 1000 daily pressures toward mediocrity.

**Implication:** Distinctiveness = costly. Most companies fail because cost of standing apart exceeds perceived value. Winners pay that cost.

## Consulting & Business Phases
Business lifecycle considerations:
- **Growth phase:** Act quickly, take risks. Speed > precision.
- **Protection phase:** Act slowly, preserve capital. Security > opportunity.

Most entrepreneurs reverse these instinctively.

## Free vs. Paid Economics
When price → $0:
- Demand becomes irrational and infinite
- Systems collapse under weight
- Incentive structures break
- Scarcity disappears, abundance paradoxically becomes liability

Example: Free healthcare (NHS waiting lists 9mo-1yr, 7M waiting); Free college (scammers filled seats).

**Lesson:** Marginal cost structure determines behavior. Price communicates scarcity; price = $0 communicates unlimited supply.

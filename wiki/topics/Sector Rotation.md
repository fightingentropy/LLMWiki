## Overview
Sector rotation reflects changing growth expectations and market regime. Different sectors outperform in different environments.

## Sector Categories

### Cyclical Sectors
Sensitive to economic cycle; outperform when growth expected:
- **Industrials**: Capital spending, manufacturing, construction
- **Materials**: Commodity-linked, mining, chemical production
- **Energy**: Oil/gas, power generation
- **Financials**: Interest rates, credit growth, risk appetite
- **Consumer Discretionary**: Spending sensitive to confidence and cash flow

### Defensive Sectors
Stable earnings, outperform when growth concerns:
- **Consumer Staples**: Necessities, non-discretionary spending
- **Healthcare**: Steady demand, demographics support, less cyclical
- **Utilities**: Essential services, regulated returns, bond-like
- **Real Estate/REIT**: Inflation hedge, stable yields

### Neutral / Structural Growth
- **Technology**: Partially cyclical, partially structural; depends on whether it's capex boom or caution
- **Telecom**: Bond-like characteristics, stable dividends
- **Energy transition**: Structural growth but cyclically-sensitive

## Rotation Patterns

### Early Cycle (Recovery)
- **When**: Exiting recession, growth accelerating
- **Outperform**: Cyclicals, especially Industrials and Materials
- **Underperform**: Defensives (no longer needed as insurance)
- **Signal**: Cyclicals sharply outperforming defensives

### Mid-Cycle (Expansion)
- **When**: Sustained growth, no recession fears
- **Outperform**: Cyclicals continue, but dispersion narrows
- **Leadership shift**: Tech/growth may start outperforming as capex and earnings growth accelerate
- **Signal**: Broad market strength, most sectors advancing

### Late Cycle (Overheating)
- **When**: Growth too fast, inflation rising, Fed tightening
- **Outperform**: Energy (inflation), Materials (commodity strength)
- **Underperform**: Long-duration growth/tech (rate sensitivity), rate-sensitive defensives
- **Signal**: Energy leading, tech underperforming despite earnings growth

### Recession/Contraction
- **When**: Growth slowing, recession feared
- **Outperform**: Defensives (recession insurance), staples (must-buy)
- **Underperform**: Cyclicals (earnings falling)
- **Signal**: Defensive rally, cyclical collapse

## Practical Rotation Trades

### Momentum Approach
Trade the rotation that's happening:
- If cyclicals outperforming = momentum continues until sentiment shifts
- If defensives outperforming = growth concerns intact, stay defensive

### Mean Reversion Approach
Rotate when ratios extreme:
- Cyclicals/Defensives ratio at extremes = rotation risk
- Value/Growth ratio at extremes = rotation likely

### Data-Driven Approach
Follow the data that drives rotation:
- PMI accelerating = cyclicals due
- Earnings growth slowing = cyclicals due for pullback
- Credit spreads widening = defensives due

## Key Metrics to Watch
- **Cyclical/Defensive ratio**: Chart this explicitly
- **Earnings revisions**: Earnings going up = cyclical strength likely
- **Economic surprises**: Beats = cyclicals, misses = defensives
- **Fed tone**: Tightening = defensives, easing = cyclicals

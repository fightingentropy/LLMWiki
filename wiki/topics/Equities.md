## Overview
Equities are the highest-risk, highest-return asset class and most sensitive to growth expectations.

## Key Analysis Dimensions

### Index Level & Breadth
- **Index moves**: SPX, Nasdaq, Russell 2000 level
- **Advance/decline ratio**: % of stocks advancing vs. declining; deteriorating breadth = weakness ahead
- **Breadth indicators**: Concentration risk if few stocks driving whole index

### Leadership & Sector Rotation
- **Mega-cap tech dominance**: If concentrated in 7-10 names = concentration risk
- **Cyclicals vs. defensives**: Rotation shows growth expectations
- **Cyclical outperformance**: Growth expected (rates up, earnings growth expected)
- **Defensive outperformance**: Growth concerns (rates down, recession expectations)

### Valuation
- **P/E ratios**: High valuations = less margin of safety, more downside in correction
- **Earnings yield**: Inverse of P/E; compare to bond yields for relative attractiveness
- **PEG ratio**: P/E relative to growth rate; adjusted for expected growth
- **Forward guidance**: Company earnings guidance reflects management expectations

### Growth Sensitivity
- **Earnings sensitivity**: Tech/growth more sensitive to rates than value
- **Duration analogy**: High-growth stocks act like long-duration bonds; rate-sensitive
- **Multiple compression**: When rates rise, growth multiples compress (even if earnings hold)

## Equity-Specific Flows
- **Buyback season**: Q1 has most buybacks = price support late in quarter
- **Index rebalancing**: When rebalancing dates hit, predetermined flows hit
- **Options expiry**: Monthly/weekly options expiries can create gamma hedging flows

## Recession Indicators
- **Earnings revisions**: Falling forward estimates = recession risk
- **Credit spreads widening**: Companies having trouble refinancing = trouble ahead
- **Unemployment rising**: First serious sign of recession
- **Valuations contracting**: Multiple compression = earnings down forecasts

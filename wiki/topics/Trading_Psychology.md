# Trading Psychology

## Why People Trade
- Greatest game with millions of variables, no single optimal solution
- Fever dream of constantly-just-out-of-reach puzzle
- Arrogance + disdain for authority → no boss + autonomy
- Asymmetric upside ceiling vs. downside floor
- Performative, endless, introspective, disciplinary, humbling, asymmetrically rewarding
- Family destiny (line of traders before you)

## Capital Allocation Timing
"You act too quickly when trying to grow capital, and you act too slowly when trying to protect capital."

This asymmetry explains most losses—panic selling and hesitant buying.

## Market Psychology
**When news impacts price, market participants fixate on whether it's true or not. The actual veracity is usually immaterial. What matters is how the market reacts and for how long.**

**Markets are 6-8 months ahead of news.** Price moves first; explanation follows. This makes correlation studies misleading—they're reading the past, not the future.

## Reflexive Market Structure
"Reflexive pumps don't begin with wave of buyers; they start with **no one left willing to sell**. Stubborn holder base creates synthetic supply shock. Shorts pile in aggressively. Disbelief rally erupts from sideline capital waiting for dip that never comes. FOMO chases price into thin air with zero marginal sellers and infinite shorts to cover. Parabola and attention games begin."

## Cash as Option
**Cash = optionality.** Option value maximized by volatility. Inflation is option premium.

Bitcoin/crypto volatility exposure creates capital allocators well-equipped for future uncertainty and volatility.

## High-Timeframe vs. Acute Flows
ATH corrections driven by acute short-term flows ($10B+ shizo day traders) overwhelming passive flows ($500m+ ETF buying). Eventually passive flows win. Optionality value of holding cash during 40%+ wicks on alts is valuable.

"Few more liquidations and then up only."

## Intuitive Decision-Making
Intuitive traders thrive when plugged in, on top of game, processing tons of information constantly. They make preternaturally good calls.

**As they age and lose info-flow, confident inferences go awry.** This explains why genius and madness ride closely—Nobel laureates, Hughes, Elon go insane over time. More measured, careful thinkers safer from this trap. [[Genius_and_Madness]]

## Overthinking Paralysis
Overthinking + underacting = miserable traders. System: **just move.** Imperfect action beats perfect planning.

## Seasonality
- **January → October:** Robust trading activity
- **Late August → December:** Most of year's moves happen except summer doldrums
- **Week after July 4:** Nadir of trading activity
- **September:** Year really begins (post-summer); FOMC meetings = wild gyrations
- **May-August:** "Sell in May and Go Away" period

## Market Efficiency
Everything is priced in. Your existence was valued decades ago by market omniscience. Free will illusion. This is market theology—tongue-in-cheek but contains truth about how forward-looking pricing works.

## Decision vs. Outcome
Market outcome ≠ quality of decision. Randomness masks poor reasoning; luck masks bad strategy. Long-term separates signal from noise.

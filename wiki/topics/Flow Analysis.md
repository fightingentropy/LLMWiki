## Overview
Money flows are what actually move prices. Understanding who is buying and selling reveals regime changes before they show up in prices.

## Types of Flows

### Cash Flows
- **ETF inflows/outflows**: Retail buying/selling via funds
- **Insider transactions**: Company insiders buying = confidence, selling = concern
- **Buyback activity**: Companies buying own stock = price support but flushes capital
- **Redemptions**: Mutual fund outflows = forced selling

### Leverage Flows
- **Margin buying**: Increasing use of leverage = risk-taking = bulls winning
- **Margin liquidation**: Forced selling from margin calls = price decline acceleration
- **Short covering**: Shorts buying to close = squeeze
- **Short selling**: New shorts = bearish positioning

### Crypto-Specific Flows
- **Stablecoin flows**: USDT/USDC movement into/out of exchanges = positioning indicator
- **Stablecoin yields**: Borrow rates on stables = funding pressure indicator
- **Exchange inflows**: Coins moving to exchanges = selling pressure likely
- **Exchange outflows**: Coins moving to self-custody = holding conviction

### Cross-Asset Flows
- **Risk-on flow**: Money rotating into equities = away from bonds/gold
- **Risk-off flow**: Money rotating into bonds/gold = away from equities
- **Sector flows**: Within equities, rotation from one sector to another
- **EM flows**: Capital in/out of emerging markets = currency moves

## Flow Monitoring Tools
- **Qwantify**: Real-time market flow analysis
- **CoinGlass**: Liquidation flows and positioning in crypto
- **On-chain explorers**: Track wallet movements and exchange flows
- **Visa/Mastercard data**: Spending proxy for retail activity
- **Sentiment indicators**: Flow often precedes sentiment shift

## Flow Analysis Framework

### Normal Market
- Steady bidding and offering
- Flows balanced; no extreme directionality
- Prices move on news and data
- Technical levels hold

### Flow Driven Market
- Directional flow in one direction
- Buyers/sellers exhausting each other
- Prices move faster than normal on given news
- Technical levels break

### Squeeze Market
- Extreme positioning forces flows
- Liquidations cascade
- Prices gap through levels
- Momentum accelerates then reverses sharply

## Reading Flow Direction
- **Ask prices hitting**: More sellers than buyers
- **Bid prices hitting**: More buyers than sellers
- **Volume with direction**: Trending direction with high volume = strong flow
- **Volume against trend**: Exhaustion if buying into weakness without moving prices

## Practical Applications

### Identifying Regime Shifts
- Stablecoin outflows = money leaving risk assets = bearish
- ETF inflows to growth = rotation into risk = bullish
- Insider selling spike = executives concerned = caution

### Catching Extremes
- Margin at extremes = leverage ready to blow
- Shorts at extremes = squeeze risk high
- Long positions clustered = rotation risk
- Options extremes = gamma edge to one side

### Predicting Moves
- Flow acceleration = move continuation likely
- Flow reversal = inflection likely
- Flow divergence = new money entering vs. old holders leaving

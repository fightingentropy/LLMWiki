## Overview
Silver is a hybrid asset: industrial demand like copper, but safe-haven characteristics like gold. More volatile than gold due to smaller market size.

## Key Drivers

### Industrial Demand
- **Electronics**: Conductivity and reliability; shrinking but structural
- **Solar panels**: Grid-scale solar requires significant silver; growing
- **Photography & other**: Legacy and niche uses

### Precious Metals / Safe-Haven
- **Investor flows**: ETFs, funds, safe-haven positioning
- **Safe-haven value**: Insurance demand in risk-off periods
- **Speculation**: Leverage and positioning can amplify moves

### Supply-Side
- **Primary mining**: Dedicated silver mining is niche; most is by-product
- **By-product supply**: Silver extracted from copper, zinc, lead mining
- **Recycling**: Electronics and industrial recycling supply
- **Mine economics**: By-product status means supply doesn't respond quickly to price

### Macro Factors
- **Real yields**: Like gold, inverse to real rates
- **USD strength**: Weak dollar = higher silver (cheaper in foreign currencies)
- **Gold-Silver ratio**: Relative value between precious metals

## Trading Characteristics
- **More volatile than gold**: Smaller market = bigger moves on same flows
- **Industrial cycle**: Coupled to industrial production and solar demand
- **Positioning leverage**: Futures positioning can drive sharp reversals
- **Mean reversion**: High gold-silver ratios tend to compress over time

## The Gold-Silver Ratio
- **What it is**: Price of gold ÷ price of silver (ratio of ounces)
- **High ratio**: Silver cheap relative to gold; history suggests it compresses
- **Low ratio**: Silver expensive relative to gold; could be overvalued
- **Mean reversion play**: Extremes often reverse

## Scenarios
- **Risk-off**: Both rallies, but gold rallies more (safe-haven dominant over industrial)
- **Growth surge**: Silver outperforms gold (industrial demand kicks in)
- **Real rate spike**: Both fall, but silver falls harder (leveraged inverse real yield)
- **Tech boom**: Silver rallies (electronics, solar demand)

# Technology Critique

The wiki's scattered-but-consistent suspicion of tech-optimization culture. Not Luddite — the wiki is full of tools — but alert to the ways tools reshape the wielder.

## In this wiki

Introduced in [[Culture]] under "Technology Critique & Women," extended through [[Thoughts]] on spatial awareness, information density, and AI-as-mirror.

## Key themes

- **Tools shape the wielder.** "Young women's resistance to technology is not irrational. Women investigate the dynamic of how tools mend and shape the wielder more than men do. There's wisdom in questioning tech optimization culture that makes the internet worse" ([[Culture]]).
- **Hatred-of-capitalism chain.** Hatred of capitalism → billionaires → tech billionaires → tech. Male-dominated tech-bro culture is a contributing factor ([[Culture]]).
- **Podcasts = lowest information density.** "Podcasts have the lowest information density of any medium ever created, but they're totally passive. Unlike reading, require zero energy. Partly why they're horrible for learning" ([[Thoughts]]).
- **Spatial awareness decline.** "Steady increase in people seemingly unaware of existence in 3D space. Listlessly loitering in doorways, shocked when someone says 'excuse me.' Not rude — genuinely confused." Technology rewires spatial cognition ([[Thoughts]]).
- **AI punctures human exceptionalism.** "AI exposes creativity as remixing, insight as regurgitation, expertise as memorized heuristics" ([[Thoughts]]).
- **Work theater economy.** "We've built an entire shadow economy around simulation of work rather than work itself. Productivity-industrial complex feeds on desire to feel progress rather than make it" ([[Thoughts]]) — optimization tools as paralysis disguised as momentum.
- **Sterile optimization.** [[Self_Improvement_Culture_Critique]] extends this into the Huberman/Ferriss/Fridman axis — tools for performance disconnected from generativity.

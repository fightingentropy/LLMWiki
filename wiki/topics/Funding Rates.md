## Overview
Perpetual futures funding rates are a direct measure of leverage and leverage imbalance in crypto markets. They reveal when longs or shorts are paying to hold positions.

## How Funding Rates Work

### Mechanics
In perpetual futures (perpetual swap):
- No expiration date like traditional futures
- Traders can hold positions indefinitely
- To balance longs and shorts, there's a funding rate
- Whoever is on the crowded side (usually longs) pays the uncrowded side (usually shorts)

### Positive Funding Rate
- **Meaning**: Longs are paying shorts
- **Implication**: More longs than shorts = crowding on long side
- **Market signal**: Bulls dominating, risk-on sentiment

### Negative Funding Rate
- **Meaning**: Shorts are paying longs
- **Implication**: More shorts than longs = crowding on short side
- **Market signal**: Bears dominating, risk-off sentiment (rare)

### Extreme Funding
- **0.1%+ per day**: 36%+ annualized = bubble risk
- **0.2%+ per day**: 73%+ annualized = dangerous crowding

## Funding Rate Signals

### Rising Positive Funding
- Longs accumulating
- Leverage building on long side
- Bubble risk increasing
- Exhaustion warning

### Falling or Negative Funding
- Longs capitulating
- Leverage reducing
- Shorts accumulating (contrarian indicator)
- Setup for potential bounce

### Zero or Balanced Funding
- No crowding
- Balanced positioning
- Neutral market
- Vulnerable to directional move

## Trading Implications

### High Positive Funding (>0.05% per day)
- **Setup**: Long-biased market with leverage
- **Risk**: Liquidations on sharp selloff
- **Trade**: Short pullbacks (fade rallies) or wait for cascade
- **Probability**: Eventual capitulation

### Negative Funding (Rare)
- **Setup**: Unusual positioning; extreme bearishness
- **Opportunity**: Short squeeze setup
- **Trade**: Long into weakness or wait for reversal
- **Probability**: Oversold bounce

### Rapidly Rising Funding
- **Setup**: Trend day up, longs piling in
- **Risk**: That's the last rally
- **Trade**: Short into momentum
- **Setup**: Climactic action

## Relationship to Price

### Funding vs. Price
- High funding doesn't predict direction, only crowding
- But crowding limits upside (buyers exhausted)
- And increases downside risk (forced selling)
- Extreme funding often precedes consolidation then reversal

### Cascades Often Happen at High Funding
- Prices trending up on high funding
- Something triggers uncertainty
- Longs start to worry
- Early longs exit, hitting others' stops
- Cascade begins

[Strategy](https://x.com/0xaporia/status/1894297482500141122) hopping is one of the most under discussed issues on CT imo. It's mostly a flight from the grueling, nonlinear phase where most progress happens.

All strategies, even brilliant ones, operate in a landscape of randomness and friction. When people encounter a losing streak, they interpret it as proof that their strategy is flawed, rather than recognizing that noise is part of the system.

Suppose a strategy has a 50% success rate—a coin flip. Over 100 trades, the probability of experiencing a losing streak:

• of 5 or more is approx. 80%

• of 6 or more is approx. 55%

• of 7 or more is approx. 32%

• of 8 or more is approx. 17%

Now, suppose your strategy has a 40% success rate—closer to reality. Over 100 trades, the probability of experiencing a losing streak:

• of 5 or more is approx. 98%

• of 6 or more is approx. 87%

• of 7 or more is approx. 69%

• of 8 or more is approx. 49%

Losing streaks are not rare events. Yet, humans are terrible at internalizing this. We intuitively expect outcomes to balance out quickly, not grasping that streaks are inherent to randomness.

A surgeon with a 95% success rate will eventually lose multiple patients in a row. A poker player should focus on whether they made mathematically optimal decisions, not whether they won the hand.

**Decouple Process from Outcome.**

# Retatrutide Reconstitution Guide: From Powder to Injection

> ⚠️ **Important Disclaimer:** Retatrutide (LY3437943) is an **investigational compound** still in Phase 3 clinical trials. It is **not FDA-approved**. This information is for educational and research reference purposes only. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider.

## 📋 Supplies You'll Need

| Supply | Details |
|---|---|
| **Retatrutide vial** | Lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder, typically 5mg, 10mg, or 30mg |

| **Sharps container** | For safe needle disposal |
| **Clean workspace** | Wiped down, flat surface |

### The Math Formula (Do It Yourself)

$$\text{Concentration} = \frac{\text{Peptide Amount (mcg)}}{\text{Water Added (mL)}}$$

$$\text{Units to inject} = \frac{\text{Desired Dose (mcg)}}{\text{Concentration (mcg/mL)}} \times 100$$

**Example:** 10,000 mcg vial + 2 mL water = 5,000 mcg/mL. For a 4 mg (4,000 mcg) dose: $4{,}000 \div 5{,}000 \times 100 = 80$ units.

## 🧪 Step-by-Step Reconstitution

### Step 1: Bring to Room Temperature
Let the lyophilized retatrutide vial and BAC water sit out for **15–20 minutes** so they reach room temperature. Cold powder is harder to dissolve.

### Step 2: Clean Everything
- **Wash your hands** thoroughly with soap and water for 20+ seconds
- **Wipe both rubber stoppers** (retatrutide vial + BAC water vial) with alcohol swabs
- Let them **air dry** for 10–15 seconds — do not blow on them

### Step 3: Draw Bacteriostatic Water
- Using a clean syringe, pull back the plunger to draw air equal to your target volume
- Insert needle into BAC water vial and **inject the air** (equalizes pressure)
- Invert the vial and **slowly draw** your calculated amount of BAC water
- Remove air bubbles by gently tapping and pushing plunger until a drop appears at the tip

### Step 4: Add Water to the Peptide Vial ⚠️ CRITICAL STEP
- Insert the needle into the retatrutide vial
- **Angle the needle toward the glass wall** — NOT directly at the powder
- **Slowly depress the plunger** over 10–20 seconds, letting the water trickle down the inside wall
- **Never blast water directly onto the powder** — this can denature/damage the peptide

### Step 5: Mix Gently — DO NOT SHAKE
- **Gently roll** the vial between your palms, or **swirl slowly** in circular motions
- The powder should dissolve within **1–3 minutes** into a **clear, colorless** solution
- If not fully dissolved, let it sit in the fridge for 10–30 minutes and check again
- **NEVER shake** — shaking creates foam and air bubbles that destroy peptide bonds (can reduce potency 30–50%)

### Step 6: Inspect the Solution
Hold the vial up to light. It should be:
- ✅ **Completely clear** — no visible particles
- ✅ **Colorless** (slight yellow tint is acceptable)
- ❌ If **cloudy** or has **floating particles** → discard (may be contaminated or degraded)

### Step 7: Label & Refrigerate Immediately
Write on the vial:
- Compound name & vial size (e.g., "Retatrutide 10mg")
- Concentration (e.g., "5 mg/mL")
- Date reconstituted
- Use-by date (28 days from reconstitution)

## 🧊 Storage Guidelines

| State | Temperature | Shelf Life |
|---|---|---|
| Lyophilized (powder) | Room temp | 6–12 months |
| Lyophilized (powder) | Refrigerated (2–8°C) | 2+ years |
| Lyophilized (powder) | Frozen (-20°C) | 3+ years |
| **Reconstituted (BAC water)** | **Refrigerated (2–8°C)** | **Up to 28 days** |
| Reconstituted (sterile water only) | Refrigerated | Use within 24 hours |

> 🚫 **Never freeze** reconstituted peptide — ice crystals shear peptide bonds apart. Only freeze lyophilized powder.
> 🚫 **Never leave** reconstituted solution at room temperature — loses potency rapidly.

## 💉 Injection Quick Guide

- **Route:** Subcutaneous (SubQ) only
- **Frequency:** Once weekly, same day each week
- **Injection sites:** Abdomen (most common), front of thigh, or back of upper arm
- **Rotate sites** each injection (at least 1 inch from last site)
- **Needle angle:** 45°–90° depending on body composition and needle length
- **Pinch** a fold of skin, insert needle, push plunger slowly over 5–10 seconds
- Let the vial sit at room temp for **15–30 minutes** before drawing your dose (cold solution causes more discomfort)

## ❌ Common Mistakes That Ruin Your Peptide

| Mistake | Consequence |
|---|---|
| **Shaking the vial** | Denatures peptide, reduces potency 30–50% |
| **Spraying water onto powder** | Damages peptide molecular structure |
| **Using sterile water instead of BAC water** | No preservative = must use within 24 hours |
| **Leaving reconstituted peptide at room temp** | Loses 10–20% potency per day |
| **Freezing reconstituted solution** | Ice crystals destroy peptide bonds |
| **Reusing needles** | Contamination risk + dulls needle |
| **Adding too little water** | Ultra-concentrated = impossible to measure small doses accurately |

<details>
<summary>📖 Why Bacteriostatic Water (Not Sterile Water)?</summary>

BAC water contains **0.9% benzyl alcohol** as a preservative that inhibits bacterial growth. This is what allows you to store a reconstituted vial for up to **28 days** and draw multiple doses from it.

**Sterile water for injection (SWI)** has NO preservative — once you puncture the vial, bacteria can grow rapidly. SWI-reconstituted peptides must be used within **24 hours** and discarded.

Since retatrutide is dosed once weekly and a single vial spans multiple weeks, **BAC water is essential**.

⚠️ **Benzyl alcohol allergy:** A small percentage of people are sensitive to benzyl alcohol. If you have this allergy, you must use sterile water and reconstitute fresh for each dose.

</details>
